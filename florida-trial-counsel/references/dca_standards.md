# Florida District Courts of Appeal - Controlling Standards

## Overview

Florida has six District Courts of Appeal (DCAs). Each DCA's decisions are binding within its district and persuasive in other districts. The Florida Supreme Court resolves inter-district conflicts.

## Standards of Review

### De Novo Review
Applied to:
- Pure questions of law
- Constitutional issues
- Statutory interpretation
- Summary judgment rulings
- Motions to dismiss
- Jury instructions (legal correctness)

**Standard:** No deference to trial court; appellate court decides the issue independently.

### Abuse of Discretion
Applied to:
- Discovery rulings
- Evidentiary rulings
- Sanctions
- Continuances
- New trial motions
- Attorney's fees determinations

**Standard:** Reversal only if the trial court's decision was "arbitrary, fanciful, or unreasonable" or "no reasonable person would agree." *Canakaris v. Canakaris*, 382 So. 2d 1197, 1203 (Fla. 1980).

### Competent Substantial Evidence
Applied to:
- Factual findings after non-jury trial
- Administrative agency decisions

**Standard:** Affirm if any competent substantial evidence supports the finding, even if other evidence supports a contrary finding. *Dept. of Highway Safety v. Critchfield*, 842 So. 2d 782 (Fla. 2003).

### Mixed Questions of Law and Fact
Applied to:
- Application of law to facts
- Contract interpretation (when extrinsic evidence used)

**Standard:** De novo for legal conclusions; deferential for underlying factual findings.

## Key Procedural Standards

### Motion to Dismiss (Rule 1.140(b)(6))

**Standard:** Accept all factual allegations as true; view in light most favorable to plaintiff. Dismiss only if complaint fails to state a cause of action.

**Key Cases:**
- *Siegle v. Progressive Consumers Ins. Co.*, 819 So. 2d 732 (Fla. 2002)
- *De Vaux v. Westwood Baptist Church*, 953 So. 2d 677 (Fla. 1st DCA 2007)
- *Ginsberg v. Lennar Fla. Holdings, Inc.*, 645 So. 2d 490 (Fla. 3d DCA 1994)

### Summary Judgment (Rule 1.510)

**Standard (Post-2021 Amendment):** Movant must show no genuine dispute of material fact and entitlement to judgment as a matter of law. If met, burden shifts to nonmovant to demonstrate genuine dispute.

**Key Cases:**
- *In re Amendments to Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510*, 309 So. 3d 192 (Fla. 2020)
- *Wilsonart, LLC v. Lopez*, 308 So. 3d 961 (Fla. 2020)
- *Celotex Corp. v. Catrett*, 477 U.S. 317 (1986) (now applicable in Florida)

### Temporary Injunction

**Standard:** Four-factor test:
1. Likelihood of success on the merits
2. Irreparable harm absent injunction
3. Balance of hardships favors movant
4. Injunction serves the public interest

**Key Cases:**
- *Gainesville Woman Care, LLC v. State*, 210 So. 3d 1243 (Fla. 2017)
- *Naegele Outdoor Advert., Inc. v. City of Jacksonville*, 659 So. 2d 1046 (Fla. 1995)

### Discovery Disputes

**Standard:** Abuse of discretion. Courts have broad discretion over discovery.

**Key Cases:**
- *Allstate Ins. Co. v. Langston*, 655 So. 2d 91 (Fla. 1995)
- *Dodson v. Persell*, 390 So. 2d 704 (Fla. 1980)

### Attorney's Fees

**Standard:** Finding of entitlement reviewed de novo; amount reviewed for abuse of discretion.

**Key Cases:**
- *Baratta v. Valley Oak Homeowners' Ass'n*, 891 So. 2d 1063 (Fla. 2d DCA 2004)
- *Florida Patient's Comp. Fund v. Rowe*, 472 So. 2d 1145 (Fla. 1985) (Rowe factors)

## Substantive Law Standards by DCA

### Contract Interpretation

**General Rule:** Contract interpretation is a question of law reviewed de novo when based solely on the written document. When extrinsic evidence is considered, factual findings are reviewed for competent substantial evidence.

**1st DCA:**
- *Acceleration Nat'l Serv. Corp. v. Brickell Fin. Servs. Motor Club*, 541 So. 2d 738 (Fla. 1st DCA 1989)

**2nd DCA:**
- *Friedman v. Virginia Metal Prods. Corp.*, 56 So. 2d 515 (Fla. 2d DCA 1952)

**3rd DCA:**
- *Llano Fin. Grp., LLC v. Fermata Inv. II, LLC*, 227 So. 3d 1243 (Fla. 3d DCA 2017)

**4th DCA:**
- *Friedman v. New York Life Ins. Co.*, 985 So. 2d 56 (Fla. 4th DCA 2008)

**5th DCA:**
- *Accelerated Benefits Corp. v. Peaslee*, 858 So. 2d 1096 (Fla. 5th DCA 2003)

### Negligence

**Duty:** Question of law (de novo).
**Breach, Causation, Damages:** Questions of fact (competent substantial evidence if bench trial; jury verdict not to be disturbed if supported by evidence).

**Florida Supreme Court:**
- *Clay Elec. Coop., Inc. v. Johnson*, 873 So. 2d 1182 (Fla. 2003)
- *McCain v. Fla. Power Corp.*, 593 So. 2d 500 (Fla. 1992) (foreseeability as duty element)

### Fraud

**Standard:** Must be pled with particularity (Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.120(b)). Elements are questions of fact, but legal sufficiency of pleading is reviewed de novo.

**Key Cases:**
- *Lopez-Infante v. Union Cent. Life Ins. Co.*, 809 So. 2d 13 (Fla. 3d DCA 2002)
- *Johnson v. Davis*, 480 So. 2d 625 (Fla. 1985) (fraud in real estate transactions)

### Breach of Fiduciary Duty

**Elements:**
1. Fiduciary relationship exists
2. Breach of duty
3. Proximate causation
4. Damages

**Key Cases:**
- *Doe v. Evans*, 814 So. 2d 370 (Fla. 2002)
- *Capital Bank v. MVB, Inc.*, 644 So. 2d 515 (Fla. 3d DCA 1994)

## Preservation of Error

### General Rule
To preserve an issue for appeal, a party must:
1. Make a timely and specific objection
2. State the legal ground for the objection
3. Obtain a ruling from the trial court

**Key Case:** *Castor v. State*, 365 So. 2d 701 (Fla. 1978)

### Exceptions (Fundamental Error)
The following may be raised for the first time on appeal:
- Lack of subject matter jurisdiction
- Constitutional errors affecting fundamental rights (rare in civil cases)
- Void judgments

### Motion for Directed Verdict / JMOL
Must be made at close of opposing party's evidence and renewed at close of all evidence to preserve sufficiency of evidence challenge.

### Post-Trial Motions
File motion for new trial or motion to alter/amend judgment within **15 days** to preserve certain issues.

## Inter-District Conflicts

When DCAs disagree on an issue, the Florida Supreme Court has discretionary jurisdiction to resolve the conflict. Fla. Const. art. V, ยง 3(b)(3).

**Practice Tip:** When no controlling authority exists in your DCA, cite persuasive authority from other DCAs but note any potential conflicts.

---

## Certified Questions

DCAs may certify questions of great public importance to the Florida Supreme Court. Fla. R. App. P. 9.030(a)(2)(A)(v).

**Practice Tip:** If your case involves a novel issue, consider requesting certification.
