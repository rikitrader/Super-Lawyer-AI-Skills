## 4.1 Hearing Mechanics

### NOTICE OF HEARING
**PURPOSE:** Notify parties of scheduled hearing
**WHEN TO USE:** After obtaining hearing time from court
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Required for all hearings; specifies motion to be heard
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Date/time/location; specific motion identified (Rule 1.100)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.100(b)

### MOTION TO SET FOR HEARING
**PURPOSE:** Request court set hearing on pending motion
**WHEN TO USE:** When motion filed but no hearing set
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Move case forward; get ruling
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of pending motion; proposed dates
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.100

### MOTION TO CONTINUE HEARING
**PURPOSE:** Postpone scheduled hearing
**WHEN TO USE:** Conflict; need more time; witness unavailable
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Obtain additional preparation time
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; proposed new dates; opposing counsel position
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.460

### MOTION TO CONTINUE TRIAL
**PURPOSE:** Postpone scheduled trial
**WHEN TO USE:** Discovery incomplete; witness unavailable; settlement pending
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Major decision; courts reluctant to continue
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Strong good cause; diligence shown; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.460

### MOTION TO SHORTEN TIME
**PURPOSE:** Reduce notice period for hearing
**WHEN TO USE:** Emergency or urgent matter; insufficient time under rules
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Expedited relief
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause for shortened time; urgency
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.090(d)

### MOTION FOR ENLARGEMENT OF TIME
**PURPOSE:** Extend deadline for filing or response
**WHEN TO USE:** Before deadline expires; good cause exists
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent default/waiver; obtain needed time
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; diligence; proposed new deadline
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.090(b)

### JOINT STIPULATION TO EXTEND DEADLINES
**PURPOSE:** Agreed extension of case deadlines
**WHEN TO USE:** Parties agree to more time; court approval needed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Cooperative case management; avoid disputes
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Both parties' signatures; proposed new deadlines
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.090(b)

### MOTION TO APPEAR BY ZOOM / TELEPHONIC APPEARANCE
**PURPOSE:** Request remote hearing attendance
**WHEN TO USE:** Travel burden; health issues; attorney convenience
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Reduce costs; accommodate scheduling
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Reason for remote appearance; technology availability
**RULE REFERENCE:** Local rules; Administrative Orders

### PROPOSED ORDER
**PURPOSE:** Provide court with proposed ruling
**WHEN TO USE:** With motion or after hearing
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Shape court's ruling; save court time
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Complete proposed order; blanks for date/signature
**RULE REFERENCE:** Local practice

## 4.2 Case Management

### MOTION FOR CASE MANAGEMENT CONFERENCE
**PURPOSE:** Request conference to address case administration
**WHEN TO USE:** Complex case; discovery disputes; scheduling issues
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Court involvement in case progress; resolve disputes
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Issues to be addressed; proposed agenda
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### CASE MANAGEMENT REPORT
**PURPOSE:** Inform court of case status
**WHEN TO USE:** As required by case management order; before conference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Demonstrate progress; identify obstacles
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Status of discovery; pending motions; trial readiness
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### JOINT CASE MANAGEMENT REPORT
**PURPOSE:** Both parties' agreed case status report
**WHEN TO USE:** Before case management conference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Show cooperation; identify disputes
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Joint preparation; signatures
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### MOTION TO SET TRIAL
**PURPOSE:** Request trial date
**WHEN TO USE:** Discovery complete; case ready for trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Move toward resolution; create deadline pressure
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Certificate of readiness; time estimate
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.440

### NOTICE OF TRIAL
**PURPOSE:** Notify parties of trial setting
**WHEN TO USE:** After court sets trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Official trial notice; starts pretrial deadlines
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Trial date/time/location
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.440

### MOTION TO EXPEDITE
**PURPOSE:** Request accelerated case schedule
**WHEN TO USE:** Urgency exists; standard timeline inadequate
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Faster resolution; compressed deadlines
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause for expedition; proposed schedule
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### MOTION TO STAY PROCEEDINGS
**PURPOSE:** Halt case temporarily
**WHEN TO USE:** Pending appeal; arbitration; bankruptcy; related case
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve status quo; await other proceedings
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal basis for stay; prejudice if denied
**RULE REFERENCE:** Court's inherent authority

### MOTION TO LIFT STAY
**PURPOSE:** Resume previously stayed proceedings
**WHEN TO USE:** Stay basis resolved; ready to proceed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Resume litigation; move toward resolution
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Status of underlying issue; readiness to proceed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Court's inherent authority

## 4.3 Dismissal (Non-Dispositive)

### NOTICE OF VOLUNTARY DISMISSAL
**PURPOSE:** Dismiss own claim without prejudice
**WHEN TO USE:** Before answer/MSJ; once as of right
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Strategic withdrawal; preserve re-filing right
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Notice only (no court approval for first voluntary)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(a)(1)

### STIPULATION OF DISMISSAL
**PURPOSE:** Agreed dismissal
**WHEN TO USE:** Settlement; mutual agreement to end case
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Clean resolution; terms as agreed
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Both parties' signatures; with/without prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(a)(1)

### MOTION FOR VOLUNTARY DISMISSAL
**PURPOSE:** Seek dismissal after losing right to dismiss as of right
**WHEN TO USE:** After answer/MSJ filed; court approval needed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Requires court approval; may have conditions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; proposed terms (with/without prejudice)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(a)(2)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR LACK OF PROSECUTION
**PURPOSE:** Dismiss case for plaintiff's failure to prosecute
**WHEN TO USE:** One year without record activity (Rule 1.420(e))
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** End stale case; no retrial on merits
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Lack of activity for one year; notice served
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(e)

### NOTICE OF RECORD ACTIVITY
**PURPOSE:** Avoid dismissal for lack of prosecution
**WHEN TO USE:** Within one year; to prevent 1.420(e) dismissal
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Keep case alive; reset clock
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Description of activity; filing constitutes activity
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(e)

## 4.4 Track Assignment (Rule 1.200)

### MOTION TO ASSIGN TO STREAMLINED TRACK
**PURPOSE:** Request simplified procedures for less complex case
**WHEN TO USE:** Case suitable for expedited handling
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Faster resolution; limited discovery
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Case complexity analysis; suitability for track
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200(b)

### MOTION TO ASSIGN TO COMPLEX TRACK
**PURPOSE:** Request enhanced procedures for complex case
**WHEN TO USE:** Multiple parties; complex issues; extended discovery needed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** More resources; dedicated judge; extended timeline
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Complexity factors; why standard track inadequate
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200(c)

### OBJECTION TO TRACK ASSIGNMENT
**PURPOSE:** Challenge assigned case track
**WHEN TO USE:** Track inappropriate for case characteristics
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Adjust procedures to fit case needs
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Why current track inappropriate; proposed track
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

## 4.5 Scheduling

### MOTION TO MODIFY SCHEDULING ORDER
**PURPOSE:** Change deadlines in scheduling order
**WHEN TO USE:** Good cause to extend or modify deadlines
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Adjust timeline; accommodate changes
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; proposed modifications; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### MOTION FOR PREFERENTIAL TRIAL SETTING
**PURPOSE:** Request priority trial date
**WHEN TO USE:** Party over 70; terminal illness; statutory preference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Accelerated trial; jump queue
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Statutory or equitable basis; prejudice if delayed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. ยง 40.50

### MOTION TO CONSOLIDATE CASES
**PURPOSE:** Combine related cases for joint handling
**WHEN TO USE:** Common questions of law/fact; judicial economy
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Efficiency; consistent rulings
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Common issues; efficiency; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.270(a)

### MOTION TO SEVER CLAIMS OR PARTIES
**PURPOSE:** Separate claims or parties for independent handling
**WHEN TO USE:** Confusion, delay, or prejudice from joinder
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Cleaner trial; separate issues
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Reasons for severance; prejudice from joint trial
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.270(b)

## Case Management Checklist

### Initial Phase
- [ ] Note answer deadline (20 days)
- [ ] Calendar initial disclosure deadline (60 days)
- [ ] Note case management conference date
- [ ] Identify applicable track (streamlined/general/complex)
- [ ] Note discovery cutoff
- [ ] Note expert disclosure deadlines

### Ongoing Management
- [ ] Monitor one-year prosecution deadline (Rule 1.420(e))
- [ ] Calendar all hearing dates
- [ ] File record activity if approaching one year
- [ ] Comply with case management orders
- [ ] Supplement disclosures as needed
- [ ] Prepare case management reports
