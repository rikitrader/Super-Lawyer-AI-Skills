## The Dispositive Motion Pipeline

Dispositive motions are where cases are WON or LOST before trial. Master this category.

### Strategic Framework

```
1. DEFAULT PATH
   Request Clerk's Default → Motion for Final Default Judgment

2. PLEADING PATH
   Motion to Dismiss → Judgment on Pleadings (if sustained with prejudice)

3. SUMMARY JUDGMENT PATH
   Motion for Summary Judgment → Rule 1.510 practice

4. SANCTION PATH
   Motion for Discovery Sanctions → Motion to Dismiss as Sanction
```

---

## 9.1 Default Judgments

### REQUEST FOR CLERK'S DEFAULT
**PURPOSE:** Establish defendant failed to respond within time allowed
**WHEN TO USE:** 20+ days after service; no response filed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** First step to default judgment; freezes defendant's right to defend
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Proof of service; affidavit of non-response; military affidavit (SCRA)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.500(a)

### MOTION FOR FINAL DEFAULT JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Obtain judgment against defaulting defendant
**WHEN TO USE:** After clerk's default entered; for liquidated or unliquidated damages
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Win case without trial; collect immediately
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Clerk's default; proof of damages; proposed judgment
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.500(e)

### MOTION FOR DEFAULT JUDGMENT ON LIABILITY WITH DAMAGES HEARING
**PURPOSE:** Establish liability by default; set hearing on damages
**WHEN TO USE:** Unliquidated damages requiring proof (personal injury, etc.)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Liability established; only damages contested
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Clerk's default; notice of hearing; damage evidence ready
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.500(e)

### MOTION TO SET ASIDE DEFAULT
**PURPOSE:** Vacate clerk's default before final judgment
**WHEN TO USE:** Good cause shown; meritorious defense exists
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Restore right to defend; typically requires showing excusable neglect
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Affidavit of meritorious defense; explanation of delay; proposed answer
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.500(d)

### MOTION TO SET ASIDE DEFAULT JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Vacate final default judgment
**WHEN TO USE:** Excusable neglect, fraud, or void judgment
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Second chance to defend; high burden to meet
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Rule 1.540(b) grounds; meritorious defense; diligence shown
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.540(b)

## 9.2 Summary Judgment - Movant

### MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Obtain judgment as matter of law; no genuine dispute of material fact
**WHEN TO USE:** After discovery reveals no triable issues; 40 days before trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Win without trial; tremendous leverage
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Statement of undisputed facts; supporting evidence; legal argument
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a)

### MOTION FOR PARTIAL SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Obtain judgment on some but not all claims/defenses
**WHEN TO USE:** Some claims clearly established; narrow issues for trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Reduce trial scope; establish key elements
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Same as full MSJ; specify claims/defenses targeted
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a)

### MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT ON LIABILITY
**PURPOSE:** Establish liability as matter of law; reserve damages for trial
**WHEN TO USE:** Liability undisputed; damages require determination
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Liability locked in; trial only on damages
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Evidence of each liability element; no material disputes
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a)

### MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT ON DAMAGES
**PURPOSE:** Obtain judgment on damages amount
**WHEN TO USE:** Liability established; damages certain or calculable
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Complete judgment without damages trial
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Damage calculations; supporting documentation
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a)

### MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT ON AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
**PURPOSE:** Defeat specific affirmative defense as matter of law
**WHEN TO USE:** Defense fails legally or factually
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate defense; simplify trial
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Evidence defeating each defense element
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a)

### REPLY IN SUPPORT OF MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Address opponent's opposition; highlight insufficient response
**WHEN TO USE:** After opposition filed; optional but often persuasive
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Last word; address new arguments; show no genuine dispute
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Response to each opposition argument; reply evidence if needed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510

### SUPPLEMENT TO MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Add evidence discovered after filing MSJ
**WHEN TO USE:** New evidence supports motion; before hearing
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Strengthen record; address weaknesses
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** New evidence; explanation of relevance
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510

## 9.3 Summary Judgment - Opponent

### RESPONSE IN OPPOSITION TO MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Defeat summary judgment by showing genuine dispute
**WHEN TO USE:** Within time allowed (typically 20 days or per court order)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve right to trial; create factual issues
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Counter-statement of disputed facts; opposing evidence; legal argument
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(c)

### STATEMENT OF DISPUTED MATERIAL FACTS
**PURPOSE:** Identify genuine disputes requiring trial
**WHEN TO USE:** With opposition to MSJ
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Frame factual disputes; show jury questions exist
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Cite evidence for each disputed fact
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(c)

### MOTION TO STRIKE SUMMARY JUDGMENT EVIDENCE
**PURPOSE:** Exclude inadmissible evidence from MSJ consideration
**WHEN TO USE:** When MSJ relies on hearsay, unauthenticated documents, etc.
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Weaken movant's evidentiary support
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of inadmissible evidence; legal basis for exclusion
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(e)

### MOTION TO CONTINUE SUMMARY JUDGMENT FOR ADDITIONAL DISCOVERY
**PURPOSE:** Postpone MSJ ruling pending needed discovery
**WHEN TO USE:** Discovery incomplete; facts in opponent's control
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve right to develop record
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Affidavit showing what discovery needed; relevance; diligence
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(d)

### MOTION FOR EVIDENTIARY HEARING ON SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Request hearing to assess evidence credibility
**WHEN TO USE:** Credibility central to disputed facts
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Rare but available in exceptional cases
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Showing why credibility determination needed pre-trial
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510

### MOTION TO EXCLUDE AFFIDAVIT AS NOT BASED ON PERSONAL KNOWLEDGE
**PURPOSE:** Strike affidavit lacking foundation
**WHEN TO USE:** Affiant lacks firsthand knowledge; conclusory statements
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate key MSJ evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Analysis of affidavit deficiencies
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(e)

### MOTION TO EXCLUDE HEARSAY BUSINESS RECORDS AT SUMMARY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Strike records lacking proper foundation
**WHEN TO USE:** Business records not properly authenticated; hearsay
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Critical in banking/foreclosure cases
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Analysis of foundation deficiencies; hearsay objections
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.803(6)

## 9.4 Judgment on the Pleadings

### MOTION FOR JUDGMENT ON THE PLEADINGS
**PURPOSE:** Obtain judgment based solely on pleadings
**WHEN TO USE:** After pleadings closed; no outside evidence needed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Early case resolution; pleadings establish entitlement
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument from pleading contents
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(c)

### RESPONSE IN OPPOSITION TO JUDGMENT ON THE PLEADINGS
**PURPOSE:** Defeat motion by showing disputed issues
**WHEN TO USE:** Within response time
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve trial right
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Argument why pleadings show triable issues
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(c)

## 9.5 Dismissal Motions (Dispositive)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR FAILURE TO STATE A CAUSE OF ACTION
**PURPOSE:** Challenge legal sufficiency of complaint
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of service; or with answer
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** End case early; avoid discovery
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument why elements not alleged
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(6)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR LACK OF SUBJECT MATTER JURISDICTION
**PURPOSE:** Challenge court's power to hear case
**WHEN TO USE:** Any time (cannot be waived)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Fundamental defect; case cannot proceed
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument on jurisdictional limits
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(1)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR LACK OF PERSONAL JURISDICTION
**PURPOSE:** Challenge court's power over defendant
**WHEN TO USE:** Before or with answer (waived if not timely)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Force re-filing in proper forum
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Affidavit of lack of contacts; legal argument
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(2)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR IMPROPER VENUE
**PURPOSE:** Challenge case location
**WHEN TO USE:** Before or with answer (waivable)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Transfer to more favorable forum
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Argument on proper venue under Fla. Stat. § 47
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(3)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR FAILURE TO JOIN INDISPENSABLE PARTY
**PURPOSE:** Challenge absence of required party
**WHEN TO USE:** When necessary party not included
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Force joinder or dismissal
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of party; why indispensable
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)(7)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS
**PURPOSE:** Bar time-barred claims
**WHEN TO USE:** When SOL apparent from complaint face
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Complete defense to claim
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** SOL calculation; accrual date argument
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)

### MOTION TO DISMISS FOR RES JUDICATA / COLLATERAL ESTOPPEL
**PURPOSE:** Bar claims already adjudicated
**WHEN TO USE:** Prior judgment on same claims/issues
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Absolute bar; prevents re-litigation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Prior judgment; same parties/issues analysis
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(b)

### MOTION TO DISMISS AS SANCTION FOR DISCOVERY VIOLATIONS
**PURPOSE:** Ultimate sanction for discovery abuse
**WHEN TO USE:** Repeated willful violations; lesser sanctions failed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Case-ending sanction
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** History of violations; prejudice; lesser sanctions exhausted
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380(b)(2)

## 9.6 Arbitration-Related Dispositive

### MOTION TO COMPEL ARBITRATION AND STAY PROCEEDINGS
**PURPOSE:** Enforce arbitration agreement; halt litigation
**WHEN TO USE:** Valid arbitration clause exists
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Move to arbitral forum; potential cost/speed advantage
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Arbitration agreement; scope analysis
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 682

### MOTION TO ABATE / STAY PENDING ARBITRATION
**PURPOSE:** Pause case during arbitration
**WHEN TO USE:** After arbitration compelled
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve litigation rights post-arbitration
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Pending arbitration; scope of stay
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 682

### MOTION TO CONFIRM ARBITRATION AWARD
**PURPOSE:** Convert arbitration award to court judgment
**WHEN TO USE:** After final arbitration award
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Make award enforceable as judgment
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Arbitration award; proper service
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 682.12

### MOTION TO VACATE ARBITRATION AWARD
**PURPOSE:** Challenge arbitration award
**WHEN TO USE:** Fraud, corruption, evident partiality, misconduct
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Undo unfavorable arbitration result
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Grounds under Fla. Stat. § 682.13
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 682.13

## 9.7 Involuntary Dismissal

### MOTION FOR INVOLUNTARY DISMISSAL
**PURPOSE:** Dismiss plaintiff's case at close of plaintiff's evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** Bench trial; plaintiff failed to prove case
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Win without presenting defense
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument why plaintiff's proof insufficient
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.420(b)

### MOTION FOR DIRECTED VERDICT
**PURPOSE:** Obtain verdict as matter of law during jury trial
**WHEN TO USE:** After plaintiff rests; or after all evidence
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Win without jury deliberation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** No reasonable jury could find for opponent
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.480

## Dispositive Motion Checklist

### Summary Judgment (Moving Party)
- [ ] Discovery sufficient to support motion
- [ ] Statement of undisputed material facts prepared
- [ ] Each fact supported by admissible evidence
- [ ] Legal argument on each element
- [ ] Filed 40+ days before trial (or per order)
- [ ] Proposed order attached
- [ ] Hearing requested

### Summary Judgment (Opposing Party)
- [ ] Response filed timely
- [ ] Each "undisputed" fact addressed
- [ ] Disputed facts identified with evidence
- [ ] Evidentiary objections preserved
- [ ] Rule 1.510(d) continuance if discovery incomplete
- [ ] Cross-motion considered if appropriate
