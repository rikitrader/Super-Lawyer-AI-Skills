## 2.1 Initial Pleadings

### COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Initiate civil action stating claims and relief sought
**WHEN TO USE:** Commencement of lawsuit; within applicable SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Frames entire litigation; establishes burden allocation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Ultimate facts for each element; exhibits (contracts, etc.)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### VERIFIED COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Complaint with sworn verification of facts
**WHEN TO USE:** When facts within personal knowledge; fraud claims; injunction requests
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Adds evidentiary weight; may support emergency relief
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Verification page signed under oath; notarization
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.030(a)

### AMENDED COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Modify original complaint (add claims, parties, facts)
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days as of right; after leave of court
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Cure deficiencies; add discovered claims; respond to MTD
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Motion for leave (if past 20 days); proposed amended pleading
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(a)

### SECOND AMENDED COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Further modification after first amendment
**WHEN TO USE:** When additional amendments needed; new facts discovered
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Final opportunity to perfect pleading; courts scrutinize more closely
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Motion for leave; good cause showing; no prejudice to opponent
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(a)

### THIRD AMENDED COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Third modification of complaint
**WHEN TO USE:** Rare; only with court permission and strong justification
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Courts highly skeptical; must show exceptional circumstances
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Detailed good cause; explanation of new facts; prejudice analysis
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(a)

## 2.2 Responsive Pleadings

### ANSWER
**PURPOSE:** Respond to complaint allegations; assert defenses
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of service (or 10 days after MTD denied)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Admits/denies allegations; preserves affirmative defenses
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Paragraph-by-paragraph responses; affirmative defenses pled
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(a)(1)

### AMENDED ANSWER
**PURPOSE:** Modify original answer
**WHEN TO USE:** When additional defenses discovered; to correct errors
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Add affirmative defenses; correct admissions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Motion for leave (if past 20 days); proposed amended answer
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(a)

### AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES
**PURPOSE:** Assert defenses that defeat plaintiff's claims even if allegations true
**WHEN TO USE:** In Answer; must be specifically pled
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Shifts issues; may defeat claims entirely
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Ultimate facts supporting each defense
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(d)

### COUNTERCLAIM
**PURPOSE:** Assert defendant's claims against plaintiff
**WHEN TO USE:** In Answer; compulsory if arises from same transaction
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Offensive posture; may exceed original claim value
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Same as complaint requirements; elements and facts
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.170(a)

### CROSSCLAIM
**PURPOSE:** Assert claims against co-party (co-defendant or co-plaintiff)
**WHEN TO USE:** When claims exist between parties on same side
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Establish contribution/indemnity rights; shift blame
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Same as complaint requirements
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.170(g)

### THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Bring in new party who may be liable to defendant
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of answer or by leave of court
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Spread liability; ensure all responsible parties present
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Motion for leave (if late); impleader complaint
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.180(a)

### REPLY TO AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES
**PURPOSE:** Respond to defendant's affirmative defenses
**WHEN TO USE:** Optional; or when ordered by court
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Challenge defenses; avoid deemed admissions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific responses to each defense
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.100(a)

### REPLY TO COUNTERCLAIM
**PURPOSE:** Answer defendant's counterclaim
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of counterclaim service
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Deny counterclaim allegations; assert defenses
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Same as answer requirements
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(a)(1)

### ANSWER TO CROSSCLAIM
**PURPOSE:** Respond to co-party's crossclaim
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of crossclaim service
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve defenses against co-party
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Paragraph responses; defenses
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(a)(1)

### ANSWER TO THIRD-PARTY COMPLAINT
**PURPOSE:** Third-party defendant's response to impleader
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 20 days of third-party complaint service
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Challenge basis for impleader; assert defenses
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Full answer with affirmative defenses
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(a)(1)

## 2.3 Amendment Motions

### MOTION FOR LEAVE TO AMEND PLEADING
**PURPOSE:** Seek court permission to file amended pleading
**WHEN TO USE:** After 20-day period; or for second/subsequent amendments
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Cure deficiencies; add claims/defenses; respond to rulings
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Proposed amended pleading; good cause; no prejudice to opponent
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(a)

### MOTION TO CONFORM PLEADINGS TO EVIDENCE
**PURPOSE:** Amend pleadings to match evidence presented at trial
**WHEN TO USE:** During or after trial when evidence exceeds pleadings
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve claims/defenses proven but not pled
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of evidence; proposed conforming amendment
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(b)

### MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE SUPPLEMENTAL PLEADING
**PURPOSE:** Add allegations of events occurring after original pleading
**WHEN TO USE:** When new facts arise during litigation
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Address continuing conduct; update damage claims
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Description of new facts; proposed supplemental pleading
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.190(c)

## 2.4 Pleading Challenges

### MOTION TO STRIKE SHAM PLEADING
**PURPOSE:** Strike pleading that contradicts prior sworn statements
**WHEN TO USE:** When amended pleading contradicts prior testimony/pleading without explanation
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminates false defenses; exposes dishonesty
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Prior inconsistent statement; comparison showing sham
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.150

### MOTION FOR MORE DEFINITE STATEMENT
**PURPOSE:** Require clearer pleading before responding
**WHEN TO USE:** When pleading so vague response cannot be framed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Force specificity; clarify claims before answer
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific identification of vague allegations
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(e)

### MOTION TO STRIKE
**PURPOSE:** Remove insufficient defense, redundant, immaterial, impertinent, or scandalous matter
**WHEN TO USE:** When pleading contains improper matter
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Clean up pleadings; remove prejudicial content
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of objectionable matter; legal basis
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(f)

### MOTION TO STRIKE AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES
**PURPOSE:** Strike legally insufficient affirmative defenses
**WHEN TO USE:** When defenses conclusory or legally invalid
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Narrow issues; eliminate unsupported defenses
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument why each defense fails
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(f)

### MOTION TO STRIKE COUNTERCLAIM
**PURPOSE:** Strike legally insufficient counterclaim
**WHEN TO USE:** When counterclaim fails to state cause of action
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate defendant's offensive claims
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal argument why counterclaim fails
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.140(f)

## 2.5 Specialized Complaints by Claim Type

### COMPLAINT FOR BREACH OF CONTRACT
**PURPOSE:** Sue for contract breach damages
**WHEN TO USE:** Written or oral contract breach; 5-year/4-year SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Core commercial litigation claim
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Contract (or terms); breach identification; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR BREACH OF WRITTEN CONTRACT
**PURPOSE:** Sue on written contract breach
**WHEN TO USE:** Written contract exists; 5-year SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Written contract presumed valid; clearer proof
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Attached written contract; breach; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR BREACH OF ORAL CONTRACT
**PURPOSE:** Sue on verbal agreement breach
**WHEN TO USE:** No written contract; 4-year SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Higher burden; must prove terms existed
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Evidence of agreement terms; performance; breach
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENCE
**PURPOSE:** Sue for damages from negligent conduct
**WHEN TO USE:** Duty, breach, causation, damages present; 4-year SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Broad tort claim; comparative fault applies
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Duty facts; breach facts; causation; damage proof
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR FRAUD
**PURPOSE:** Sue for damages from fraudulent misrepresentation
**WHEN TO USE:** Intentional misrepresentation causing reliance damages; 4-year SOL
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Punitive damages available; must plead with particularity
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Who/what/when/where/why/how; reliance; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.120(b) (particularity)

### COMPLAINT FOR NEGLIGENT MISREPRESENTATION
**PURPOSE:** Sue for damages from careless false statements
**WHEN TO USE:** Professional or business relationship; reliance on false info
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Easier standard than fraud; no intent required
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** False statement; duty; breach of duty; reliance; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR UNJUST ENRICHMENT
**PURPOSE:** Equitable claim for benefit conferred without payment
**WHEN TO USE:** No valid contract; defendant retained benefit
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Alternative to contract claim; prevents windfall
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Benefit conferred; knowledge; inequity of retention
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR QUANTUM MERUIT
**PURPOSE:** Sue for reasonable value of services rendered
**WHEN TO USE:** Services provided; no express contract; expectation of payment
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Recovery even without contract
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Services rendered; value; defendant's benefit
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR ACCOUNT STATED
**PURPOSE:** Sue on agreed account balance
**WHEN TO USE:** Prior dealings; balance struck and agreed; no timely objection
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Streamlined proof; agreement on amount
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Prior dealings; statement sent; no objection; balance due
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR DECLARATORY JUDGMENT
**PURPOSE:** Seek court declaration of parties' rights/obligations
**WHEN TO USE:** Actual controversy exists; need guidance before breach
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Proactive litigation; clarify rights before harm
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Justiciable controversy; affected rights
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. ยง 86.011

### COMPLAINT FOR INJUNCTIVE RELIEF
**PURPOSE:** Seek court order compelling or prohibiting conduct
**WHEN TO USE:** Irreparable harm; no adequate legal remedy
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Immediate relief; change behavior before trial
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Irreparable harm; likelihood of success; balance of hardships
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.610

### COMPLAINT FOR SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE
**PURPOSE:** Seek court order compelling contract performance
**WHEN TO USE:** Unique subject matter (land, rare goods); damages inadequate
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Force completion of deal; equitable remedy
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Valid contract; plaintiff's performance; damages inadequate
**RULE REFERENCE:** Equity principles

### COMPLAINT FOR TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH CONTRACT
**PURPOSE:** Sue third party who induced contract breach
**WHEN TO USE:** Third party caused breach; damages resulted
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Reach beyond contracting parties
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Valid contract; defendant's knowledge; intentional interference; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR TORTIOUS INTERFERENCE WITH BUSINESS RELATIONSHIP
**PURPOSE:** Sue for interference with prospective business
**WHEN TO USE:** Interference with business expectancy
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect business relationships; broader than contract interference
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Business relationship; defendant's knowledge; improper interference; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR BREACH OF FIDUCIARY DUTY
**PURPOSE:** Sue fiduciary for breach of duty
**WHEN TO USE:** Trust relationship existed; duty breached
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Higher duty; potential for punitive damages
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Fiduciary relationship; duty; breach; damages
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR CIVIL THEFT (FLORIDA STATUTE 772.11)
**PURPOSE:** Sue for treble damages for theft
**WHEN TO USE:** Criminal theft elements met; demand letter sent
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Treble damages; attorney's fees; powerful remedy
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Theft elements; written demand; 30-day wait period
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. ยง 772.11

### COMPLAINT FOR CONVERSION
**PURPOSE:** Sue for wrongful exercise of control over property
**WHEN TO USE:** Defendant took/used plaintiff's property without right
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Recover value of converted property
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Ownership; wrongful taking/detention; demand if applicable
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR REPLEVIN
**PURPOSE:** Recover specific personal property
**WHEN TO USE:** Defendant possesses plaintiff's property wrongfully
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Get actual property back (not just money)
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Description of property; wrongful detention; bond
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. ยง 78.01

### COMPLAINT FOR DEFAMATION
**PURPOSE:** Sue for false statements harming reputation
**WHEN TO USE:** False statement published; damages to reputation
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Restore reputation; recover damages
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** False statement; publication; damages (or per se)
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

### COMPLAINT FOR SLANDER OF TITLE
**PURPOSE:** Sue for false statements disparaging property title
**WHEN TO USE:** False recording or statement clouding title
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Clear title; recover damages from cloud
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** False statement; publication; damages; malice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.110(b)

## Completion Checklist for Pleadings

- [ ] Caption correct (parties, case number, division)
- [ ] Parties properly identified with capacity
- [ ] Jurisdiction and venue alleged
- [ ] Ultimate facts for each element
- [ ] Each count properly labeled
- [ ] Conditions precedent alleged (Rule 1.120(c))
- [ ] Fraud pled with particularity if applicable (Rule 1.120(b))
- [ ] Demand for relief (ad damnum)
- [ ] Jury demand if applicable
- [ ] Signature block and Bar number
- [ ] Certificate of service
- [ ] Verification if required
- [ ] Exhibits attached if referenced
