## Pretrial Strategic Framework

```
PRETRIAL PIPELINE:
1. Motions in Limine → Exclude harmful evidence
2. Witness/Exhibit Lists → Disclose trial evidence
3. Jury Instructions → Frame legal issues
4. Pretrial Stipulation → Narrow disputes
5. Pretrial Conference → Final preparation
```

## 11.1 Motions in Limine

### MOTION IN LIMINE (GENERAL)
**PURPOSE:** Exclude evidence before trial begins
**WHEN TO USE:** Pretrial; prevent prejudicial evidence mention
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Avoid bell that can't be unrung
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal basis for exclusion; specific evidence targeted
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.104

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE HEARSAY RECORDS
**PURPOSE:** Prevent admission of hearsay business records
**WHEN TO USE:** Records lack foundation; improper exceptions claimed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate key proof (common in banking cases)
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Hearsay analysis; foundation deficiencies
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.802, 90.803

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE UNDISCLOSED WITNESSES
**PURPOSE:** Bar witnesses not timely disclosed
**WHEN TO USE:** Opponent failed initial disclosures; surprise witnesses
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Enforce disclosure rules; prevent ambush
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Disclosure requirements; failure to disclose; prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(e)

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE UNDISCLOSED EXHIBITS
**PURPOSE:** Bar exhibits not timely disclosed
**WHEN TO USE:** Documents not in initial disclosures; not produced
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent surprise evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Disclosure timeline; failure to produce
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(e)

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE EXPERT TESTIMONY (DAUBERT)
**PURPOSE:** Challenge expert under reliability standards
**WHEN TO USE:** Expert methodology unreliable; not properly qualified
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate opponent's expert evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Daubert factors; methodology flaws
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.702

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE CHARACTER EVIDENCE
**PURPOSE:** Prevent improper character evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** Opponent seeks to prove character for propensity
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Exclude prejudicial character attacks
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Rule 90.404 analysis; not within exception
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.404

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE PRIOR BAD ACTS
**PURPOSE:** Prevent evidence of other crimes/wrongs
**WHEN TO USE:** Offered for propensity (not MIMIC purposes)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Exclude highly prejudicial evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Williams rule analysis; propensity use
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.404(2)

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE SETTLEMENT NEGOTIATIONS
**PURPOSE:** Prevent mention of settlement discussions
**WHEN TO USE:** Opponent may reference settlement offers
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect negotiations; prevent inference
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Fla. Stat. § 90.408 application
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.408

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE SUBSEQUENT REMEDIAL MEASURES
**PURPOSE:** Prevent evidence of post-incident repairs/changes
**WHEN TO USE:** Opponent offers repairs as negligence evidence
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect reasonable remediation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Fla. Stat. § 90.407 application
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.407

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE INSURANCE EVIDENCE
**PURPOSE:** Prevent mention of insurance coverage
**WHEN TO USE:** Opponent may reference liability insurance
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent jury prejudice
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Fla. Stat. § 90.411 application
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.411

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE PRIVILEGED COMMUNICATIONS
**PURPOSE:** Prevent disclosure of privileged matter
**WHEN TO USE:** Attorney-client; work product; spousal at risk
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect confidential communications
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Privilege identification; Fla. Stat. §§ 90.501-90.510
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. §§ 90.502-90.506

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE INFLAMMATORY PHOTOGRAPHS
**PURPOSE:** Prevent prejudicial visual evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** Photos more prejudicial than probative
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Reduce emotional impact
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Fla. Stat. § 90.403 balancing
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.403

### MOTION IN LIMINE REGARDING GOLDEN RULE ARGUMENT
**PURPOSE:** Prevent improper jury appeal
**WHEN TO USE:** Anticipate "put yourself in plaintiff's shoes" argument
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent emotional manipulation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Florida prohibition on golden rule
**RULE REFERENCE:** Case law (Bullock v. Branch, 130 So. 2d 74)

### MOTION IN LIMINE TO EXCLUDE REFERENCE TO "LAWSUIT ABUSE"
**PURPOSE:** Prevent prejudicial defense rhetoric
**WHEN TO USE:** Anticipate tort reform arguments
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Keep focus on facts
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Irrelevance; prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. Stat. § 90.403

### RESPONSE TO MOTION IN LIMINE
**PURPOSE:** Oppose motion to exclude evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** When opponent files MIL against your evidence
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve right to use evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Admissibility argument; relevance
**RULE REFERENCE:** Evidence Code

## 11.2 Witness and Exhibit Lists

### WITNESS LIST
**PURPOSE:** Disclose trial witnesses to court and parties
**WHEN TO USE:** Per pretrial order deadline; usually 10-30 days before trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Required for witness testimony; locked at deadline
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Name, address, subject matter of testimony
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### SUPPLEMENTAL WITNESS LIST
**PURPOSE:** Add witnesses discovered after initial list
**WHEN TO USE:** Good cause; newly discovered witnesses
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Add late witnesses; requires showing
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Explanation; newly discovered; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### EXHIBIT LIST
**PURPOSE:** Disclose trial exhibits to court and parties
**WHEN TO USE:** Per pretrial order; typically with witness list
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Required for exhibit use; locked at deadline
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Exhibit number; description; custodian
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### SUPPLEMENTAL EXHIBIT LIST
**PURPOSE:** Add exhibits discovered after initial list
**WHEN TO USE:** Good cause; newly discovered documents
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Add late exhibits; requires showing
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Explanation; newly discovered; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### OBJECTIONS TO EXHIBIT LIST
**PURPOSE:** Challenge opponent's proposed exhibits
**WHEN TO USE:** Per pretrial order; identify authentication/hearsay issues
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Identify objections before trial; streamline process
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific objection to each exhibit
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

## 11.3 Jury Instructions

### PROPOSED JURY INSTRUCTIONS
**PURPOSE:** Request specific instructions to jury
**WHEN TO USE:** Per pretrial order; before trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Frame legal issues favorably
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Standard instructions; special instructions with authority
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.470

### OBJECTIONS TO PROPOSED JURY INSTRUCTIONS
**PURPOSE:** Challenge opponent's requested instructions
**WHEN TO USE:** Per pretrial order; before jury charge conference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent harmful instructions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal basis for objection
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.470

### SPECIAL JURY INSTRUCTION
**PURPOSE:** Request non-standard instruction
**WHEN TO USE:** Standard instructions don't cover issue
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Tailor law to your case
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Legal authority; why standard inadequate
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.470

### PROPOSED VERDICT FORM
**PURPOSE:** Define questions for jury decision
**WHEN TO USE:** With jury instructions
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Frame how jury answers key questions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Clear questions; all elements covered
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.480

### OBJECTIONS TO VERDICT FORM
**PURPOSE:** Challenge opponent's proposed verdict form
**WHEN TO USE:** Before trial; at charge conference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent confusing or biased form
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific objections
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.480

## 11.4 Pretrial Stipulation and Conference

### PRETRIAL STIPULATION
**PURPOSE:** Joint statement of agreed facts and disputed issues
**WHEN TO USE:** Required before trial; per pretrial order
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Narrow trial issues; streamline presentation
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Both parties' input; agreed/disputed facts
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### PRETRIAL STATEMENT
**PURPOSE:** Party's summary of case for court
**WHEN TO USE:** Before pretrial conference
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Inform court of position; trial needs
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Claims/defenses; witnesses; exhibits; trial time
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### JOINT PRETRIAL STATEMENT
**PURPOSE:** Combined parties' pretrial submission
**WHEN TO USE:** Per court requirement
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Comprehensive case summary
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Both parties' positions; agreed matters
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

### MOTION TO AMEND PRETRIAL STIPULATION
**PURPOSE:** Change agreed matters in stipulation
**WHEN TO USE:** Error discovered; circumstances change
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Correct or update pretrial agreement
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; no prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.200

## 11.5 Voir Dire Preparation

### PROPOSED VOIR DIRE QUESTIONS
**PURPOSE:** Request specific questions for jury selection
**WHEN TO USE:** If court permits; per local practice
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Identify bias; develop juror rapport
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific questions; relevance to case
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.431

### MOTION FOR INDIVIDUAL VOIR DIRE
**PURPOSE:** Request separate questioning of jurors
**WHEN TO USE:** Sensitive issues; pretrial publicity
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** More thorough juror examination
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Need for individual questioning
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.431

### MOTION FOR EXPANDED VOIR DIRE
**PURPOSE:** Request additional time/questions for selection
**WHEN TO USE:** Complex case; need for thorough examination
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** More complete juror vetting
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Complexity; need for expanded questioning
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.431
