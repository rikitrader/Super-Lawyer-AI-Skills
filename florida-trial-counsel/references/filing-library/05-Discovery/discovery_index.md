## Discovery Strategic Framework

```
DISCOVERY ATTACK PIPELINE:
1. Initial Disclosures (60 days) → Identify scope
2. Interrogatories (30 max) → Lock testimony
3. Requests for Production → Get documents
4. Requests for Admission → Force admissions
5. Enforcement → Compel compliance / sanctions
```

---

## 5.1 Initial Disclosures

### PLAINTIFF'S INITIAL DISCLOSURES
**PURPOSE:** Mandatory disclosure of witnesses, documents, damages, insurance
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 60 days of service of initial pleading (Rule 1.280(a))
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Front-load case information; identify key evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Witness list; document list; damages computation; insurance info
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(a)(1)

### DEFENDANT'S INITIAL DISCLOSURES
**PURPOSE:** Mandatory disclosure required of all parties
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 60 days of service of initial pleading
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Reveal defense witnesses and documents; level playing field
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Same as plaintiff's disclosures
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(a)(1)

### SUPPLEMENTAL INITIAL DISCLOSURES
**PURPOSE:** Update initial disclosures with new information
**WHEN TO USE:** When new witnesses/documents identified; continuing duty
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve ability to use evidence at trial
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Updated information; explanation of changes
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(e)

## 5.2 Interrogatories

### PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT
**PURPOSE:** Written questions requiring sworn answers
**WHEN TO USE:** After initial disclosures; early case development
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Lock in defendant's version; identify witnesses/documents
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Maximum 30 interrogatories including subparts
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340

### DEFENDANT'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO PLAINTIFF
**PURPOSE:** Written questions to plaintiff
**WHEN TO USE:** After initial disclosures or with answer
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Develop defense; challenge plaintiff's case
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Maximum 30 interrogatories including subparts
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340

### PLAINTIFF'S SECOND SET OF INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Follow-up interrogatories after initial responses
**WHEN TO USE:** When first set responses raise new questions
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Drill down on issues; clarify ambiguities
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Stay within 30-interrogatory limit total
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340

### DEFENDANT'S SECOND SET OF INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Follow-up written questions
**WHEN TO USE:** After first set responses analyzed
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Target identified issues
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Total cannot exceed 30 without court approval
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340

### SUPPLEMENTAL INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Update prior interrogatory responses
**WHEN TO USE:** New information discovered; continuing duty
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Keep record current; preserve evidence use
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Identification of changes; updated answers
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(e)

### ANSWERS TO INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Sworn responses to written questions
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 30 days of service
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Creates binding admissions; usable at trial
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Verified answers; objections stated specifically
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340(a)

### OBJECTIONS TO INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Challenge improper interrogatories
**WHEN TO USE:** With answers within 30 days
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect privileged information; limit overbroad requests
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific grounds for each objection
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340(b)

## 5.3 Requests for Production

### PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION TO DEFENDANT
**PURPOSE:** Request documents and tangible things
**WHEN TO USE:** With or after interrogatories
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Obtain documentary evidence; prove case
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific categories of documents requested
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350

### DEFENDANT'S FIRST REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION TO PLAINTIFF
**PURPOSE:** Request plaintiff's documents
**WHEN TO USE:** With answer or early discovery
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Develop defense; challenge plaintiff's documents
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific document categories
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350

### PLAINTIFF'S SECOND REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION
**PURPOSE:** Follow-up document requests
**WHEN TO USE:** After initial production reviewed; gaps identified
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Complete the record; target specific documents
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific documents still needed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350

### REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION TO NON-PARTY
**PURPOSE:** Obtain documents from third parties
**WHEN TO USE:** Non-party has relevant documents
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Obtain independent evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Subpoena duces tecum format
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.351

### RESPONSE TO REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION
**PURPOSE:** Respond to document requests
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 30 days of service
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Comply while preserving objections
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Written response; document production; privilege log if applicable
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350(b)

### OBJECTIONS TO REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION
**PURPOSE:** Challenge improper document requests
**WHEN TO USE:** With response within 30 days
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect privileged/irrelevant material
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific grounds for each objection
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350(b)

### PRIVILEGE LOG
**PURPOSE:** Identify documents withheld on privilege grounds
**WHEN TO USE:** When claiming privilege over documents
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve privilege; comply with disclosure requirements
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Date, author, recipient, subject, privilege claimed for each document
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(b)(6)

## 5.4 Requests for Admission

### PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO DEFENDANT
**PURPOSE:** Establish facts without need for proof at trial
**WHEN TO USE:** After basic discovery; to narrow issues
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Conclusively establish facts; reduce trial burden
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Clear, specific requests
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370

### DEFENDANT'S FIRST REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO PLAINTIFF
**PURPOSE:** Establish facts defendant wants admitted
**WHEN TO USE:** To narrow plaintiff's claims; challenge positions
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Force plaintiff to commit or face deemed admission
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Clear, specific requests
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370

### REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION OF GENUINENESS OF DOCUMENTS
**PURPOSE:** Authenticate documents without trial testimony
**WHEN TO USE:** Before summary judgment or trial
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Streamline authentication; save witness time
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Attach copies of documents
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370

### RESPONSES TO REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
**PURPOSE:** Admit, deny, or object to RFAs
**WHEN TO USE:** Within 30 days (deemed admitted if no response)
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Careful responses avoid harmful admissions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specific response to each request
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370(a)

### MOTION TO WITHDRAW OR AMEND ADMISSION
**PURPOSE:** Change prior admission
**WHEN TO USE:** When admission incorrect; correction serves justice
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Fix harmful admissions; requires showing
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; no prejudice to opponent
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370(b)

## 5.5 Discovery Enforcement

### MOTION TO COMPEL ANSWERS TO INTERROGATORIES
**PURPOSE:** Force answers to unanswered interrogatories
**WHEN TO USE:** After good faith conferral; inadequate responses
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Obtain needed information; potential sanctions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Conferral certificate; specific deficiencies
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380(a)

### MOTION TO COMPEL PRODUCTION
**PURPOSE:** Force document production
**WHEN TO USE:** After conferral; documents not produced
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Obtain withheld documents; expose violations
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Conferral certificate; specific documents sought
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380(a)

### MOTION TO COMPEL RESPONSES TO REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
**PURPOSE:** Force responses to RFAs
**WHEN TO USE:** No response within 30 days; or to challenge inadequate responses
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Avoid deemed admissions; get clear answers
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Conferral certificate; specific RFAs at issue
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370

### MOTION TO COMPEL BETTER PRIVILEGE LOG
**PURPOSE:** Require adequate privilege log
**WHEN TO USE:** Log insufficient to assess privilege claims
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Test privilege claims; expose improper withholding
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Deficiencies in current log; what's needed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(b)(6)

### MOTION TO DEEM MATTERS ADMITTED
**PURPOSE:** Establish facts as admitted due to non-response
**WHEN TO USE:** No response to RFAs within 30 days
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Conclusively establish facts; devastating at MSJ
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Proof of service; no response received
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370(a)

### MOTION FOR PROTECTIVE ORDER
**PURPOSE:** Limit discovery scope or manner
**WHEN TO USE:** Discovery harassing, overbroad, privileged, or burdensome
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Protect client; limit opponent's access
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Good cause; specific protection sought
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(c)

### MOTION FOR SANCTIONS FOR DISCOVERY VIOLATIONS
**PURPOSE:** Punish discovery abuse
**WHEN TO USE:** Willful violations; bad faith conduct
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Attorney's fees; adverse inference; dismissal/default
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Pattern of violations; prejudice; prior attempts to resolve
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380(b)

### MOTION TO PRECLUDE EVIDENCE FOR FAILURE TO DISCLOSE
**PURPOSE:** Exclude evidence not properly disclosed
**WHEN TO USE:** Trial; opponent failed to disclose witness/document
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Eliminate surprise evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Disclosure failure; prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280(e)

### MOTION FOR CONTINUANCE BASED ON OUTSTANDING DISCOVERY
**PURPOSE:** Delay trial/hearing pending discovery
**WHEN TO USE:** Discovery incomplete through no fault of movant
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent premature adjudication
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Discovery status; diligence; need for information
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.460

### MOTION FOR SPOLIATION SANCTIONS
**PURPOSE:** Sanction destruction of evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** Evidence destroyed after duty to preserve
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Adverse inference; cost shifting; dismissal/default
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Evidence of destruction; duty to preserve; prejudice
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380

## 5.6 ESI (Electronically Stored Information)

### NOTICE OF INTENT TO SERVE SUBPOENA FOR ESI
**PURPOSE:** Notify of intent to obtain electronic evidence
**WHEN TO USE:** Before serving ESI subpoena on non-party
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Proper procedure for electronic discovery
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Description of ESI sought; relevance
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.351

### REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION OF ESI IN NATIVE FORMAT
**PURPOSE:** Obtain electronic files in original format
**WHEN TO USE:** When metadata or native format important
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve metadata; authentic electronic evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Specification of native format requested
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350(b)

### MOTION REGARDING ESI PRESERVATION
**PURPOSE:** Address electronic evidence preservation
**WHEN TO USE:** Litigation hold issues; preservation concerns
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Prevent spoliation; ensure evidence retained
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** ESI at risk; preservation needs
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.280

### MOTION TO COMPEL ESI IN REASONABLY USABLE FORM
**PURPOSE:** Force production in accessible format
**WHEN TO USE:** ESI produced in unusable format
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Actually use electronic evidence
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Current format problems; usable format requested
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350(b)

## 5.7 Discovery Notices

### NOTICE OF SERVING DISCOVERY
**PURPOSE:** Notify court that discovery served
**WHEN TO USE:** When filing required by local rule
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Court record of discovery
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Copy of discovery served
**RULE REFERENCE:** Local rules

### NOTICE OF FILING DISCOVERY RESPONSES
**PURPOSE:** File discovery responses with court
**WHEN TO USE:** As required; or when needed for record
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Preserve record; support motions
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Responses being filed
**RULE REFERENCE:** Local rules

### CERTIFICATE OF CONFERRAL
**PURPOSE:** Certify good faith conferral before discovery motion
**WHEN TO USE:** Required before all discovery motions
**STRATEGIC IMPACT:** Mandatory prerequisite; motion denied without it
**REQUIRED SUPPORT:** Description of conferral efforts; dates; outcomes
**RULE REFERENCE:** Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.380(a)(2)

## Discovery Checklist

### Propounding Party
- [ ] Initial disclosures served (60 days)
- [ ] Interrogatories within 30-limit
- [ ] RFPs specific and relevant
- [ ] RFAs strategic (authentication, key facts)
- [ ] Responses calendared for follow-up
- [ ] Conferral before any motion
- [ ] Supplements timely served

### Responding Party
- [ ] Calendar response deadline (30 days)
- [ ] Review each request carefully
- [ ] State specific objections (not boilerplate)
- [ ] Produce documents as kept in ordinary course
- [ ] Privilege log complete
- [ ] Preserve right to supplement
- [ ] Verify answers under oath
