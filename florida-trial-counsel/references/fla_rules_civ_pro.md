# Florida Rules of Civil Procedure - Complete Reference

**Effective January 1, 2026**

## Part I: Scope and Title of Rules

### Rule 1.010 - Scope and Title
These rules govern procedure and practice in all courts of Florida in all civil actions and all special statutory proceedings, except probate, adoption, and criminal proceedings.

### Rule 1.030 - Nonverification of Pleadings
Except when otherwise specifically provided by these rules or an applicable statute, every pleading or other document of a party need not be verified or accompanied by an affidavit.

### Rule 1.040 - One Form of Action
There is one form of action known as "civil action."

---

## Part II: Commencement of Action; Service of Process

### Rule 1.050 - When Action Commenced
Every action is commenced when the complaint is filed, except that ancillary proceedings are commenced when the ancillary complaint or petition is filed.

### Rule 1.070 - Process

#### (a) Summons; Issuance
Upon commencement of the action, summons or other process authorized by law shall be issued forthwith by the clerk or judge and delivered for service.

#### (b) Service; By Whom Made
Service of process may be made by:
1. Sheriff or deputy sheriff
2. Civil process server certified under Fla. Stat. Ch. 48
3. Specially appointed person by the court
4. Certified mail (in certain circumstances)

#### (c) Service; Numerous Defendants
If a party must be served by publication and there are numerous defendants, service can be by single publication as provided by Fla. Stat. ยง 49.10.

#### (d) Service by Publication
Service by publication may be made pursuant to Fla. Stat. Ch. 49.

#### (e) Copies of Initial Pleading
At the time of personal service, the serving party must serve a copy of the initial pleading.

### Rule 1.080 - Service of Pleadings and Papers

#### (a) Service; When Required
Every pleading after the complaint, every order, every written motion (unless ex parte), and every written notice, appearance, demand, offer of judgment, designation of record on appeal, and similar document shall be served on each party.

#### (b) Service; How Made
Service shall be made by:
1. Email to the address designated in the pleading
2. Delivering a copy
3. Mailing to the last known address
4. If party has no attorney, serving the party

**Service by email is mandatory** for attorneys. (Fla. R. Gen. Prac. & Jud. Admin. 2.516)

#### (c) Service; Numerous Defendants
If numerous defendants, service shall be made as required by subdivision (b) unless the court orders otherwise.

#### (d) Filing
All documents required to be served must be filed with the court within a reasonable time after service.

## Part III: Pleadings and Motions

### Rule 1.100 - Pleadings and Motions

#### (a) Pleadings
The following are the permitted pleadings:
1. **Complaint** - initiates action
2. **Answer** - responds to complaint
3. **Counterclaim** - claim against opposing party
4. **Crossclaim** - claim against co-party
5. **Third-party complaint** - claim against non-party
6. **Reply** - responds to counterclaim (if ordered)

#### (b) Motions
An application to the court for an order shall be by motion. A motion shall state with particularity the grounds and set forth the relief sought.

### Rule 1.110 - General Rules of Pleading

#### (a) Forms of Pleadings
A pleading that states a claim shall contain:
1. **Short and plain statement** of the ultimate facts showing entitlement to relief
2. **Demand for judgment** for the relief sought

*Note: Florida uses "ultimate facts" pleading, more specific than federal notice pleading.*

#### (b) Claims for Relief
Relief in the alternative or of several different types may be demanded.

#### (c) Separate Counts
Claims founded on separate transactions or occurrences shall be stated in separate counts when a separation facilitates clear presentation.

#### (d) Defenses
Defenses shall be pleaded in short and plain terms.

#### (e) Inconsistent Claims or Defenses
A party may plead alternative statements, even if inconsistent.

#### (f) Signature of Attorney
Every pleading shall be signed by the attorney of record and include the attorney's Florida Bar number.

#### (g) Verification
When required, verification shall be by affidavit.

### Rule 1.120 - Pleading Special Matters

#### (a) Capacity
Capacity to sue or be sued need not be averred except to the extent required to show jurisdiction.

#### (b) Fraud, Mistake, Condition of Mind
Circumstances constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated with particularity. Malice, intent, knowledge, and other conditions of mind may be averred generally.

#### (c) Conditions Precedent
Performance or occurrence of conditions precedent may be averred generally.

#### (d) Official Document or Act
It is sufficient to aver that the document was issued or act done in compliance with law.

#### (e) Judgment or Decision
A judgment or decision of a domestic court or tribunal may be averred without stating matter showing jurisdiction.

#### (f) Time and Place
Time and place are material for the purpose of testing the sufficiency of a pleading.

### Rule 1.130 - Attaching Copy of Cause of Action and Exhibits

#### (a) Instruments to be Attached
When a pleading is founded on a written instrument, a copy of the instrument shall be attached to the pleading or its absence explained.

#### (b) Documents Attached
All bonds, notes, bills of exchange, contracts, accounts, or documents upon which the action may be brought shall be incorporated by reference into the pleading.

### Rule 1.140 - Defenses

#### (a) When Presented
The defendant shall file an answer within **20 days** after service of process and initial pleading on defendant.

#### (b) How Presented
The following defenses may be made by motion before pleading if a responsive pleading is permitted:
1. **(b)(1)** - Lack of jurisdiction over the subject matter
2. **(b)(2)** - Lack of jurisdiction over the person
3. **(b)(3)** - Improper venue
4. **(b)(4)** - Insufficiency of process
5. **(b)(5)** - Insufficiency of service of process
6. **(b)(6)** - Failure to state a cause of action
7. **(b)(7)** - Failure to join indispensable parties

*A motion making any of these defenses must be made before pleading if a responsive pleading is permitted.*

#### (c) Motion for More Definite Statement
If a pleading is so vague or ambiguous that a party cannot reasonably be required to frame a responsive pleading, the party may move for a more definite statement.

#### (d) Motion to Strike
A court may order stricken any redundant, immaterial, impertinent, or scandalous matter.

#### (e) Waiver
Defenses (b)(2), (b)(3), (b)(4), and (b)(5) are waived if not raised in the responsive pleading or by motion.

#### (f) Preliminary Hearings
The defenses under (b)(1)-(7) may be heard and determined before trial on application of any party.

#### (g) Consolidation
If a motion under this rule is denied, the responsive pleading shall be filed within **10 days** after notice of the court's action.

### Rule 1.150 - Sham Pleadings
If a party pleads facts that on their face appear to be sham, another party may move to strike the pleading. The court may permit or require affidavits.

### Rule 1.170 - Counterclaims and Crossclaims

#### (a) Compulsory Counterclaims
A pleading shall state as a counterclaim any claim the pleader has against any opposing party if:
1. It arises out of the transaction or occurrence that is the subject matter of the opposing party's claim
2. It does not require adding a party over whom the court cannot acquire jurisdiction

#### (b) Permissive Counterclaims
A pleading may state as a counterclaim any claim against an opposing party not arising from the same transaction or occurrence.

#### (c) Counterclaim Exceeding Opposing Claim
A counterclaim may claim relief exceeding the amount or different from that sought in the opposing party's claim.

#### (d) Crossclaim Against Co-Party
A pleading may state as a crossclaim any claim a party has against a co-party arising out of the transaction or occurrence that is the subject matter of the original action.

### Rule 1.180 - Third-Party Practice

#### (a) When Defendant May Bring in Third Party
A defendant may serve a summons and complaint on a person not a party to the action who is or may be liable for all or part of the plaintiff's claim against defendant.

#### (b) When Plaintiff May Bring in Third Party
A plaintiff may proceed under this rule against any person not a party who is or may be liable to the plaintiff for all or part of a defendant's claim against plaintiff.

### Rule 1.190 - Amended and Supplemental Pleadings

#### (a) Amendments
A party may amend a pleading once as a matter of course at any time before a responsive pleading is served. Otherwise, a party may amend only by leave of court or written consent of adverse party.

**Leave shall be freely given when justice so requires.**

#### (b) Amendments to Conform with Evidence
When issues not raised by the pleadings are tried by express or implied consent of the parties, they shall be treated as if raised in the pleadings.

#### (c) Relation Back of Amendments
An amendment relates back to the date of the original pleading when the claim asserted arises out of the conduct, transaction, or occurrence set forth in the original pleading.

#### (d) Supplemental Pleadings
A party may serve a supplemental pleading setting forth transactions or events occurring after the date of the pleading sought to be supplemented.

## Part IV: Parties

### Rule 1.210 - Parties Generally

#### (a) Capacity
A person who can sue in his or her own name may sue; a person against whom a claim exists may be sued.

#### (b) Actions by Representatives
A guardian, executor, administrator, or other fiduciary may sue or be sued without joining those for whom the person acts.

### Rule 1.220 - Class Actions

#### (a) Prerequisites
One or more members of a class may sue as representative parties if:
1. The class is so numerous that joinder is impracticable
2. There are questions of law or fact common to the class
3. The claims or defenses are typical of the class
4. The representative parties will fairly protect the interests of the class

#### (b) Certification
The court shall determine by order whether the action is to be maintained as a class action.

### Rule 1.250 - Misjoinder and Nonjoinder of Parties

#### (a) Misjoinder
Misjoinder is not ground for dismissal. Parties may be dropped or added by order of the court.

#### (b) Dropping Parties
Parties may be dropped by order of the court on its own motion or the motion of any party at any stage of the action.

## Part V: Pretrial and Case Management (2025/2026 Amendments)

### Rule 1.200 - Case Management; Pretrial Procedure

**(Substantially amended effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Case Management Tracks
Within **120 days** after the action is filed, the court shall assign the case to one of the following tracks:

**Streamlined Track:**
- Limited discovery
- Established legal issues
- Minimal anticipated motions
- Trial expected to last less than 3 days
- **Trial readiness: 12 months**

**General Track:**
- Standard civil cases
- **Trial readiness: 18 months**

**Complex Track:**
- Meets criteria under Rule 1.201
- **Trial readiness: Per court order**

#### (b) Initial Case Management Conference
The court may set an initial case management conference within the first 120 days.

#### (c) Case Management Order
The case management order shall address:
1. Discovery deadlines
2. Expert disclosure deadlines
3. Motion deadlines
4. Mediation deadline
5. Pretrial conference date
6. Trial period

**Deadlines in case management orders must be strictly enforced unless changed by court order.**

#### (d) Extension of Deadlines
Parties may submit an agreed order to extend a deadline only if the extension does not affect the ability to comply with remaining dates.

### Rule 1.201 - Complex Litigation

#### (a) Factors
A case may be designated complex if it involves:
1. Numerous pretrial motions raising difficult legal issues
2. Large number of witnesses
3. Coordination with related actions in other courts
4. Extensive or complex discovery
5. Novel legal issues
6. Substantial amounts at stake
7. Trial expected to last more than 3 days
8. Coordination with other proceedings

#### (b) Certification
A party may move for complex designation or the court may designate sua sponte.

### Rule 1.202 - Conferral Prior to Filing Motions

**(New rule effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Conferral Required
Before filing any non-dispositive motion, the movant shall confer with all parties in a good faith effort to resolve the issues raised.

#### (b) Certificate of Conferral
A motion shall include a certificate stating:
1. The date of the conferral
2. The method of conferral (in person, telephone, video)
3. The names of all participants
4. The result of conferral

#### (c) Exemptions
This rule does not apply to:
1. Dispositive motions (summary judgment, dismissal)
2. Motions for default
3. Motions for protective order sought on grounds of privilege
4. Emergency motions

## Part VI: Discovery

### Rule 1.280 - General Provisions Governing Discovery

**(Substantially amended effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Initial Disclosures
Within **60 days** after service of the complaint, each party shall disclose:

1. **Witness Information:** The name, address, and telephone number of each individual likely to have discoverable information that the disclosing party may use to support its claims or defenses

2. **Document Identification:** A copy or description of all documents, electronically stored information, and tangible things in the disclosing party's possession that the disclosing party may use to support its claims or defenses

3. **Damages Computation:** A computation of damages claimed by the disclosing party, along with documents supporting the computation

4. **Insurance:** Any insurance agreement under which an insurance business may satisfy all or part of a judgment

#### (b) Scope of Discovery
Parties may obtain discovery regarding any nonprivileged matter that is **relevant to any party's claim or defense** and **proportional to the needs of the case**, considering:

1. The importance of the issues at stake
2. The amount in controversy
3. The parties' relative access to relevant information
4. The parties' resources
5. The importance of the discovery in resolving the issues
6. Whether the burden or expense outweighs the likely benefit

*Note: Scope narrowed from "subject matter" to "claims or defenses" effective 2025.*

#### (c) Protective Orders
Upon motion and for good cause shown, the court may make any order to protect a party from annoyance, embarrassment, oppression, or undue burden or expense.

#### (d) Sequence of Discovery
Unless otherwise ordered, methods of discovery may be used in any sequence, and discovery by one party does not require any other party to delay discovery.

#### (e) Supplementation of Responses
A party who has responded to a request for discovery is under a **continuing duty** to supplement the response when:
1. The party learns that the response is incomplete or incorrect
2. The additional or corrective information has not been made known during discovery

#### (f) Limitations
The court may limit discovery if:
1. The discovery is unreasonably cumulative or duplicative
2. The discovery can be obtained from a more convenient source
3. The burden outweighs the benefit

### Rule 1.310 - Depositions Upon Oral Examination

#### (a) When Depositions May Be Taken
After commencement of the action, any party may take the testimony of any person by deposition.

#### (b) Notice of Examination
Notice shall state the time and place for taking the deposition and the name and address of each person to be examined.

**Notice period:** Reasonable notice to every other party.

#### (c) Examination and Cross-Examination
Examination and cross-examination may proceed as at trial.

#### (d) Use of Deposition
Depositions may be used:
1. To contradict or impeach the testimony of a deponent as a witness
2. For any purpose if the deponent is unavailable
3. By an adverse party for any purpose

### Rule 1.340 - Interrogatories to Parties

**(Amended effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Availability
Any party may serve written interrogatories on any other party. **Limit: 30 interrogatories**, including subparts, unless leave of court is granted.

#### (b) Answers and Objections
Answers and objections shall be served within **30 days** after service.

**Objections must state the specific grounds with detailed reasoning.** The objection must state whether any responsive materials are being withheld on the basis of the objection.

#### (c) Option to Produce Business Records
If the answer may be derived from business records, the responding party may specify the records and afford the requesting party reasonable opportunity to examine them.

### Rule 1.350 - Production of Documents and Things

**(Amended effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Scope
Any party may request production of:
1. Any designated documents
2. Electronically stored information
3. Tangible things
4. Entry on land for inspection or testing

#### (b) Procedure
The request shall set forth the items to be inspected and a reasonable time, place, and manner for inspection.

Response: Within **30 days** after service.

**Objections must specify grounds with detailed reasoning and state whether materials are being withheld.**

#### (c) Persons Not Parties
A person not a party may be compelled to produce documents and things by subpoena.

### Rule 1.360 - Examination of Persons

#### (a) When Examination May Be Ordered
When the mental or physical condition of a party is in controversy, the court may order the party to submit to an examination.

#### (b) Report of Examiner
On request, the party who obtained the examination shall deliver a copy of the examiner's detailed written report.

### Rule 1.370 - Requests for Admission

#### (a) Request for Admission
A party may serve requests that the other party admit:
1. The truth of any matters
2. The genuineness of any documents

#### (b) Response
The matter is admitted unless within **30 days** the party serves:
1. A written answer
2. An objection addressed to the matter

#### (c) Effect of Admission
Any matter admitted is conclusively established unless the court permits withdrawal or amendment.

### Rule 1.380 - Failure to Make Discovery; Sanctions

#### (a) Motion for Order Compelling Discovery
If a party fails to answer an interrogatory or respond to a request for production, the discovering party may move for an order compelling response.

#### (b) Sanctions
If a party fails to comply with an order compelling discovery, the court may:
1. Order matters taken as established
2. Refuse to allow the disobedient party to support claims or defenses
3. Prohibit introduction of designated evidence
4. Strike pleadings or parts thereof
5. Stay proceedings until the order is obeyed
6. Dismiss the action or enter default judgment
7. Hold the party in contempt

#### (c) Expenses
The court shall require the party whose conduct necessitated the motion to pay reasonable expenses, including attorney's fees.

#### (d) Failure to Attend Deposition
If a party fails to appear for deposition, the court may:
1. Order costs of the opposing party
2. Strike pleadings
3. Dismiss the action or enter default

## Part VII: Trials

### Rule 1.420 - Dismissal of Actions

#### (a) Voluntary Dismissal
**By Plaintiff:**
1. Before trial or hearing: Notice of dismissal without prejudice
2. After commencement: Only by court order

**Effect:** Unless stated otherwise, dismissal is without prejudice except that dismissal operates as an adjudication on the merits when filed by a plaintiff who has once dismissed in any court an action based on the same claim.

#### (b) Involuntary Dismissal
A defendant may move for dismissal when the plaintiff has failed to prosecute or to comply with these rules or any order of court.

#### (c) Effect of Dismissal
Unless the court specifies otherwise, a dismissal under (a)(2) and any dismissal not under this rule operates as an adjudication on the merits.

### Rule 1.430 - Demand for Jury Trial; Waiver

#### (a) Right Preserved
The right of trial by jury as declared by the Florida Constitution shall be preserved to the parties.

#### (b) Demand
Any party may demand a trial by jury by serving a written demand within **10 days** after service of the last pleading directed to the issue.

#### (c) Waiver
Failure to serve a demand waives trial by jury.

#### (d) Effect of Filing Demand
A demand may be withdrawn only with consent of all parties.

### Rule 1.440 - Setting Action for Trial

#### (a) When at Issue
An action is at issue after any motions directed to the last pleading have been disposed of.

#### (b) Notice of Trial
The clerk shall send notice of trial to all parties. The notice shall specify the date.

#### (c) Setting for Trial
If the court finds the case ready for trial, the court shall set the action for trial.

### Rule 1.460 - Continuances

**(Amended effective January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Motion for Continuance
A motion for continuance shall be in writing unless made at a trial and, except for good cause shown, shall be signed by the party requesting the continuance.

#### (b) Grounds
A continuance shall be granted only for good cause shown.

**Trial continuances should rarely be granted.** Lack of due diligence by a party is not sufficient for good cause.

#### (c) Successive Continuances
Successive continuances are highly disfavored.

#### (d) Conferral
Before filing a motion for continuance, the movant shall confer with all other parties and attempt to reach an agreement.

### Rule 1.470 - Exceptions Unnecessary; Objections

Exceptions to rulings or orders of the court are unnecessary. It is sufficient that a party states the action desired or the objection with specificity.

## Part VIII: Judgment

### Rule 1.500 - Defaults and Final Judgments Thereon

#### (a) Entry of Default
When a party against whom a judgment is sought has failed to plead or otherwise defend, the clerk shall enter a default when requested.

#### (b) Default Judgment
**By the Court:** After default, the court may enter final judgment.

**Requirements:**
1. Service must be proper
2. Affidavit of non-military service required
3. Damages must be proved at hearing

#### (c) Setting Aside Default
The court may set aside a default for good cause shown.

### Rule 1.510 - Summary Judgment

**(Substantially amended effective May 1, 2021, and January 1, 2025)**

#### (a) Motion for Summary Judgment
A party may move for summary judgment at any time after the action is at issue.

**Standard:** The judgment sought shall be rendered if there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.

#### (b) Supporting Factual Position
A party asserting no genuine dispute must support the assertion by:
1. Citing to materials in the record
2. Showing the opposing party cannot produce admissible evidence to support the fact

#### (c) Time for Motion and Response
**Motion:** May be filed at any time (check local administrative orders).
**Response:** **40 days** after service of the motion.
**Reply:** 20 days after service of the response.

*Note: Response deadline is fixed at 40 days, no longer tied to hearing date.*

#### (d) Burden of Proof
**Moving party:** Must demonstrate absence of genuine dispute as to material facts.
**Opposing party:** Must demonstrate genuine dispute by pointing to evidence in the record.

*Florida has adopted the federal Celotex/Adickes burden-shifting standards.*

#### (e) Judgment Independent of Motion
After giving notice and reasonable time to respond, the court may grant summary judgment for nonmovant, grant motion on different grounds, or grant partial summary judgment.

#### (f) Declaratory Judgment
Case not fully adjudicated on motion for summary judgment, the court may make an order specifying what material facts exist without substantial controversy.

#### (g) Time to Recover in Event of Appeal
If judgment is reversed on appeal, the court shall enter judgment for costs and attorney's fees of appeal.

### Rule 1.520 - View
The court may order a view by the jury of property or a place where a material event occurred.

### Rule 1.530 - Motions for New Trial and Rehearing; Amendments of Judgments

#### (a) Motion for New Trial
A motion for new trial shall be filed within **15 days** of the date the judgment is filed, except in eminent domain and mortgage foreclosure proceedings.

#### (b) Grounds for New Trial
New trials may be granted for:
1. Excessive or inadequate damages
2. Verdict contrary to law or evidence
3. Newly discovered evidence
4. Surprise
5. Error in proceedings

#### (c) Motion to Alter or Amend
A motion to alter or amend a judgment shall be filed within **15 days** of the date the judgment is filed.

#### (d) Timing
All motions for rehearing shall be determined within **30 days** after filing.

### Rule 1.540 - Relief from Judgment, Decrees, or Orders

#### (a) Clerical Mistakes
Clerical mistakes may be corrected by the court at any time.

#### (b) Mistakes; Inadvertence; Excusable Neglect; Newly Discovered Evidence; Fraud
On motion and upon such terms as are just, the court may relieve a party from a final judgment for:
1. Mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect
2. Newly discovered evidence
3. Fraud, misrepresentation, or misconduct
4. Void judgment
5. Judgment satisfied, released, or discharged
6. Any other justifying relief

**Time limit:** Motion must be made within a reasonable time, not more than **1 year** after judgment for grounds (1), (2), and (3).

## Part IX: Injunctions

### Rule 1.610 - Injunctions

#### (a) Temporary Injunction
A temporary injunction may be granted without notice if:
1. It appears from specific facts shown by affidavit that immediate and irreparable injury will result before the adverse party can be heard
2. The movant's attorney certifies in writing any efforts made to give notice and reasons why notice should not be required

#### (b) Bond
A temporary injunction shall be granted only after the movant gives bond in an amount fixed by the court.

#### (c) Form and Scope
Every injunction shall:
1. Be specific in terms
2. Describe the acts sought to be restrained
3. Be binding on parties and their agents

#### (d) Motion to Dissolve
A party may move to dissolve a temporary injunction at any time.
