## CROSS-EXAMINATION STRATEGIES FOR EXPERTS

### Available Attack Vectors:
1. **Qualifications** - Lack expertise in specific area
2. **Bias** - Financial, repeat business, advocacy
3. **Methodology** - Flawed, incomplete, not followed
4. **Facts** - Wrong facts, missing facts, relied on hearsay
5. **Opinion** - Not supported by data, contradicted by literature
6. **Learned Treatises** - Impeach with authoritative sources

### Principles:
- Be respectful but firm
- Use expert's own report/testimony against them
- Use learned treatises (TRE 803(18))
- Get concessions before attacking
- Don't ask questions you don't know the answer to
- Don't argue with expert - use facts

## I. BIAS AND INTEREST

**Goal:** Show expert is biased, paid advocate

### A. Financial Interest

```
Q: You're being paid to testify today?
A:

Q: What is your hourly rate?
A: $___/hour

Q: What is your total fee in this case?
A: $___

Q: You've been paid $[amount] so far?
A:

Q: And you expect to receive more?
A:

Q: You want to be paid, don't you?
A:

Q: The more hours you work, the more you're paid?
A:
```

### B. Relationship with Counsel/Party

```
Q: How many times have you been retained by [Opposing Counsel/Firm]?
A:

Q: Over how many years?
A:

Q: What percentage of your forensic work comes from [Counsel]?
A:

Q: They're a good source of business for you?
A:

Q: You'd like to continue getting referrals from them?
A:
```

### C. Advocacy Pattern

```
Q: In this case, you were retained by [Opposing Party]?
A:

Q: Your opinions favor [Opposing Party]?
A:

Q: In the last [X] cases where you testified, how many times did you testify for [mothers/fathers/petitioners/respondents]?
A:

Q: How many times did your opinion favor the party who retained you?
A:
```

## II. QUALIFICATIONS LIMITATIONS

**Goal:** Show expert lacks specific expertise for opinions given

### A. Specific Training Gaps

```
Q: Your degree is in [general field]?
A:

Q: You don't have a specific degree in [narrow specialty relevant to case]?
A:

Q: You haven't received specific training in [relevant area]?
A:

Q: You haven't published any articles on [specific issue]?
A:

Q: You're not board-certified in [specialty]?
A:
```

### B. Experience Gaps

```
Q: How many times have you [specific experience relevant to case]?
A:

Q: In how many of those did [specific circumstance] apply?
A:

Q: This is actually your first case involving [specific issue]?
A:
```

## III. METHODOLOGY ATTACKS

**Goal:** Show flawed, incomplete, or improperly applied methodology

### A. Industry Standards Not Followed

```
Q: In your field, there are accepted standards for conducting [type of evaluation]?
A:

Q: The [Association/Organization] has published guidelines?
A:

Q: Those guidelines say you should [specific requirement]?
A:

Q: But you didn't do that in this case?
A:

Q: You deviated from the accepted standard?
A:
```

### B. Incomplete Investigation

```
Q: You reviewed [X] documents?
A:

Q: You didn't review [important document]?
A:

Q: You interviewed [X] people?
A:

Q: You didn't interview [important person]?
A:

Q: You didn't [visit home/observe interaction/conduct test]?
A:

Q: That would have been helpful information?
A:

Q: But you formed your opinion without it?
A:
```

### C. Testing/Evaluation Deficiencies (for custody evaluators)

```
Q: You conducted psychological testing?
A:

Q: You used the [Test Name]?
A:

Q: That test has known limitations?
A:

Q: It can't detect [specific issue]?
A:

Q: You only observed [limited interaction]?
A:

Q: You didn't observe [full range of situations]?
A:
```

## IV. FACTUAL BASIS ATTACKS

**Goal:** Show expert relied on wrong, incomplete, or disputed facts

### A. Wrong Facts

```
Q: Your opinion is based on certain facts?
A:

Q: If those facts are wrong, your opinion could change?
A:

Q: You assumed [fact]?
A:

Q: But that's disputed in this case?
A:

Q: You weren't aware that [contradicting fact]?
A:

Q: That would be important to know?
A:
```

### B. One-Sided Information

```
Q: You spoke with [Opposing Party] for how long?
A:

Q: You spoke with [Our Client] for how long?
A:

Q: You gave [Opposing Party] more time?
A:

Q: You reviewed documents provided by [Opposing Party]?
A:

Q: Did you independently verify those documents?
A:

Q: You relied on what [Opposing Party] told you?
A:
```

### C. Hearsay Reliance

```
Q: Some of the information you relied on came from third parties?
A:

Q: You weren't present when [event] occurred?
A:

Q: You're relying on what someone told you?
A:

Q: That person isn't here to testify?
A:

Q: You can't verify what they told you?
A:
```

## V. OPINION LIMITATIONS

**Goal:** Limit scope and certainty of expert's opinions

### A. Degree of Certainty

```
Q: Your opinion isn't absolute?
A:

Q: It's possible you could be wrong?
A:

Q: There's a range of professional opinions on this issue?
A:

Q: Reasonable experts can disagree?
A:
```

### B. Alternative Explanations

```
Q: There could be other explanations for [observation]?
A:

Q: You considered [alternative]?
A:

Q: [Alternative] is also consistent with the data?
A:

Q: You can't rule out [alternative]?
A:
```

### C. Limiting Concessions

```
Q: You can't predict the future?
A:

Q: People change over time?
A:

Q: Circumstances change?
A:

Q: Your opinion is based on a snapshot in time?
A:

Q: Things could be different now?
A:
```

## VI. LEARNED TREATISE IMPEACHMENT (TRE 803(18))

**Goal:** Use authoritative texts to contradict expert

### Setup:

```
Q: Are you familiar with [Author/Publication]?
A:

Q: [Publication] is a recognized authority in your field?
A:

Q: Other professionals rely on [Publication]?
A:

[If expert won't acknowledge authority, establish through own expert or judicial notice]
```

### Impeachment:

```
Q: Let me read from [Publication], page [X]:

[Read relevant passage]

Q: That's what [Publication] says?
A:

Q: But your opinion in this case is different?
A:

Q: [Publication] contradicts your opinion?
A:
```
