## OPENING STATEMENT PRINCIPLES

**Purpose:** Tell the judge what the evidence WILL show (roadmap)

**Rules:**
- Do NOT argue
- Do NOT give opinions
- Do NOT state facts not in evidence
- DO preview the evidence
- DO establish your theme
- DO organize by issue

**Remember:** In bench trial, judge already knows the law. Focus on facts.

**Time:** _____ minutes allotted

## I. INTRODUCTION

### A. Appearance

```
Good [morning/afternoon], Your Honor.

[Name] appearing on behalf of [Petitioner/Respondent], [Client Name],
who is present in the courtroom.
```

### B. Case Overview (Theme Statement)

```
This case is about [THEME - one sentence summary].

[Or]

The evidence will show that [core narrative in 2-3 sentences].
```

| Theme Type | Example |

## OPENING STATEMENT CHECKLIST

### Content:
- [ ] Theme clearly stated
- [ ] Parties introduced
- [ ] Children introduced (if any)
- [ ] Key facts previewed
- [ ] Witnesses previewed
- [ ] Key exhibits mentioned
- [ ] Relief requested stated
- [ ] Conclusion reinforces theme

### Delivery:
- [ ] Under time limit
- [ ] No argument
- [ ] No opinions
- [ ] Only facts that will be proved
- [ ] Eye contact with judge
- [ ] Confident but not aggressive
- [ ] Notes organized for reference

### Avoid:
- [ ] "I think" or "I believe"
- [ ] Legal argument
- [ ] Inflammatory language
- [ ] Promises you can't keep
- [ ] Stating inadmissible evidence
- [ ] Personal attacks
