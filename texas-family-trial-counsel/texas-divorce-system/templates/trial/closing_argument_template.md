## CLOSING ARGUMENT PRINCIPLES

**Purpose:** Argue how the evidence supports your position and applies to the law

**NOW you can:**
- Argue
- Draw inferences
- Comment on credibility
- Apply facts to law
- Ask for specific relief

**Structure:** Issue by issue, applying evidence to legal standard

**Bench Trial Note:** Judge knows the law. Focus on how facts meet standards.

---

## II. CONSERVATORSHIP

### A. Legal Standard

```
Your Honor, the governing standard for conservatorship is the best
interest of the child. Texas Family Code Section 153.002.

The Court must consider the best interest factors, and there is a
presumption that appointment of joint managing conservators is in
the children's best interest. TFC ยง 153.131.

[Or if sole managing sought]

That presumption may be rebutted when the evidence shows [grounds].
```

**Factor 4: Parental abilities**

- [Specific evidence of parenting]
- [Specific testimony]

- [Specific evidence]

**Factor 5: Programs available**
[Evidence showed...]

## III. POSSESSION AND ACCESS

### A. Legal Standard

```
The standard possession order is presumed to be in the best interest
of the child. TFC ยง 153.252.

[Or if modified schedule requested]

The Court may deviate from the standard possession order when the
evidence establishes that a different schedule is in the child's
best interest.
```

## IV. CHILD SUPPORT

### A. Legal Standard

```
Child support is calculated under the guidelines in Texas Family Code
Chapter 154. For [one/two/three] child(ren), the guideline is
[20/25/30]% of net resources.
```

### B. Apply Evidence

```
The evidence established [Obligor]'s income:

- Pay stubs showed gross monthly income of $[amount]
- Tax returns confirmed annual income of $[amount]
- [Other income evidence]

[Obligor]'s net resources are $[amount].

[Percentage]% of net resources equals $[amount] per month.

[If deviation requested]
A deviation [above/below] guidelines is appropriate because [reasons
from evidence].
```

## V. PROPERTY DIVISION

### A. Legal Standard

```
Texas Family Code Section 7.001 requires the Court to divide the
marital estate in a manner that is just and right, having due regard
for the rights of each party.

This does not require an equal division, but a fair division
considering all circumstances.
```

## VI. SPOUSAL MAINTENANCE (if applicable)

### A. Legal Standard

```
Under Texas Family Code Section 8.051, the Court may order maintenance
if the spouse seeking maintenance:

1. Lacks sufficient property to meet minimum reasonable needs; AND
2. Meets one of the qualifying conditions:
   - Marriage of 10+ years and lacks earning ability
   - Unable to earn due to disability
   - Custodian of disabled child
   - Family violence within 2 years
```

### B. Apply Evidence

```
[If requesting]
The evidence established that [Client]:

- Was married to [Opposing Party] for [X] years
- [Qualifying condition evidence]
- Has monthly expenses of $[amount] (Exhibit [#])
- Has income of only $[amount]
- Cannot meet minimum reasonable needs

[Opposing Party] has the ability to pay because:
- [Income evidence]
- [Asset evidence]

[If opposing]
The evidence does not support maintenance because:
- [Evidence contradicting need]
- [Evidence of ability to earn]
```

### C. Specific Request

```
[If requesting]
[Client] asks the Court to order maintenance of $[amount] per month
for [X] months to [purpose - retraining, transition, etc.].

[If opposing]
[Client] asks the Court to deny spousal maintenance.
```
