     Pursuant to Rule 194 of the Texas Rules of Civil Procedure,

disclose, within thirty (30) days of service of this request, the
information and material described in Rule 194.2(a) through (l).

                         REQUEST FOR DISCLOSURE

     Please disclose the following pursuant to TRCP 194.2:

     1. The correct names of the parties to the lawsuit (TRCP 194.2(a));

     2. The name, address, and telephone number of any potential
parties (TRCP 194.2(b));

     3. The legal theories and, in general, the factual bases of
the responding party's claims or defenses (TRCP 194.2(c));

     4. The amount and any method of calculating economic damages
(TRCP 194.2(d));

     5. The name, address, and telephone number of persons having
knowledge of relevant facts, and a brief statement of each identified
person's connection with the case (TRCP 194.2(e));

     6. For any testifying expert:
          (a) the expert's name, address, and telephone number;
          (b) the subject matter on which the expert will testify;
          (c) the general substance of the expert's mental impressions
              and opinions and a brief summary of the basis for them,
              or if not retained or specially employed, the documents
              reflecting such information;
          (d) if the expert is retained or specially employed, all
              documents, tangible things, reports, models, or data
              compilations that have been provided to, reviewed by,
              or prepared by or for the expert in anticipation of
              the expert's testimony; and
          (e) the expert's current resume and bibliography
              (TRCP 194.2(f));

     7. Any discoverable indemnity and insuring agreements
(TRCP 194.2(g));

     8. Any discoverable settlement agreements (TRCP 194.2(h));

     9. Any discoverable witness statements (TRCP 194.2(i));

     10. In a suit alleging physical or mental injury and damages
from the occurrence that is the subject of the case, all medical
records and bills that are reasonably related to the injuries or
damages asserted or, in lieu thereof, an authorization permitting
the disclosure of such medical records and bills (TRCP 194.2(j));

     11. In a suit alleging physical or mental injury and damages
from the occurrence that is the subject of the case, all medical
records and bills obtained by the responding party by virtue of an
authorization furnished by the requesting party (TRCP 194.2(k));

     12. The name, address, and telephone number of any person who
may be designated as a responsible third party (TRCP 194.2(l)).
