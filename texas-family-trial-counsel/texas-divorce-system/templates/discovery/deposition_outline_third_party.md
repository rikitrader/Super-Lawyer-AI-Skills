### CATEGORY A: FAMILY MEMBER WITNESS

**Witness:** {{FAMILY_MEMBER_NAME}}
**Relationship:** {{RELATIONSHIP_TO_PARTY}}

#### A1. Background and Relationship

```
Q: Please state your name for the record.
Q: How are you related to [party]?
Q: How long have you known [party]?
Q: How long have you known [other party]?
Q: How often do you see [party]?
Q: How often do you see the children?
Q: Describe your relationship with the children.
```

#### A2. Observations of Marriage

```
Q: Did you observe the marriage between [Petitioner] and [Respondent]?
Q: What did you observe about their relationship?
Q: Did you ever witness any conflict between them?
Q: Did either party ever confide in you about marital problems?
Q: What did they tell you? [Note: may be hearsay but useful for discovery]
```

#### A3. Observations of Parenting

```
Q: Have you observed [party] with the children?
Q: Describe what you have seen.
Q: Have you observed [other party] with the children?
Q: Describe what you have seen.
Q: Who appears to be the primary caregiver?
Q: What leads you to that conclusion?
Q: Have you ever had concerns about either parent's care of the children?
```

#### A4. Specific Incidents

```
Q: Were you present on [specific date]?
Q: What did you observe?
Q: Who else was present?
Q: What was said?
Q: What happened next?
```

#### A5. Bias Assessment

```
Q: Do you have a preference for which parent should have custody?
Q: Have you discussed your testimony with [party] or their attorney?
Q: What were you told?
Q: Are you being compensated for your testimony?
Q: Do you have any personal conflict with [other party]?
```

### CATEGORY D: EXPERT WITNESS

**Witness:** {{EXPERT_NAME}}
**Specialty:** {{SPECIALTY}}

#### D1. Qualifications

```
Q: Please state your name and profession.
Q: What are your educational credentials?
Q: What licenses or certifications do you hold?
Q: How long have you been practicing in this field?
Q: Have you testified as an expert before?
Q: How many times? In what types of cases?
```

#### D2. Engagement

```
Q: Who retained you in this case?
Q: When were you retained?
Q: What were you asked to do?
Q: What is your fee?
Q: How much have you been paid so far?
Q: What materials were you provided?
Q: What materials did you review?
```

#### D3. Methodology

```
Q: What methodology did you use?
Q: Is this methodology generally accepted in your field?
Q: What data did you rely upon?
Q: Did you conduct any independent investigation?
Q: What assumptions did you make?
```

#### D4. Opinions

```
Q: What opinions have you formed in this case?
Q: What is the basis for each opinion?
Q: To what degree of certainty do you hold this opinion?
Q: What would change your opinion?
Q: Are there facts that would be inconsistent with your opinion?
```

#### D5. Report

```
Q: Did you prepare a written report?
Q: Is the report complete?
Q: Have you changed any opinions since the report?
Q: Is there anything you would add to the report?
```

#### D6. Impeachment Areas

```
Q: Have you ever given a different opinion in a similar case?
Q: Have you ever been criticized for your methodology?
Q: Have you ever had testimony excluded?
Q: Do you always testify for the party who pays you?
Q: What percentage of your income comes from expert testimony?
```

## General Deposition Tips for Third-Party Witnesses

**Approach:**
- Generally more cooperative than opposing party
- May be uncomfortable being involved
- May have loyalty to one party
- Focus on facts, not opinions (unless expert)

**Goals:**
- Lock in favorable testimony
- Discover unfavorable information
- Assess credibility
- Preserve testimony for trial

**Cautions:**
- Don't alienate neutral witnesses
- Be respectful of their time
- Get necessary facts efficiently
- Consider if deposition is even necessary vs. subpoena at trial
