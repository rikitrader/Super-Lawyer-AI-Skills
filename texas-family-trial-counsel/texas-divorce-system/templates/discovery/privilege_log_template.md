## Privilege Log Requirements (TRCP 193.3)

When withholding documents based on privilege, the responding party must:

1. **Withhold** the privileged document from production
2. **Assert** the specific privilege claimed
3. **Describe** the document sufficiently to enable assessment of the claim
4. **Produce** a privilege log with required information

**Required Information per TRCP 193.3(b):**
- Date of the document
- Author/sender
- Recipient(s)
- General subject matter
- Privilege asserted
- Basis for the privilege

## Common Privileges in Family Law

### Attorney-Client Privilege (TRE 503)

**Elements:**
1. Communication
2. Between attorney and client
3. For purpose of facilitating legal services
4. Intended to be confidential

**Common Examples:**
- Emails between client and attorney about case
- Letters from attorney to client with legal advice
- Notes from attorney-client meetings
- Draft pleadings sent for client review

**Sample Basis Language:**
```
"This document is a confidential communication between [Client]
and [his/her] attorney, [Attorney Name], made for the purpose of
obtaining legal advice concerning this matter."
```

---

### Work Product Doctrine (TRCP 192.5)

**Elements:**
1. Material prepared or mental impressions developed
2. In anticipation of litigation
3. By or for a party or party's representative

**Types:**
- **Core Work Product:** Attorney's mental impressions, conclusions, opinions (nearly absolute protection)
- **Other Work Product:** Documents prepared in anticipation of litigation (qualified protection)

**Common Examples:**
- Attorney notes and memoranda
- Legal research
- Case analysis documents
- Witness interview summaries
- Litigation strategy documents

**Sample Basis Language:**
```
"This document was prepared by counsel in anticipation of litigation
and contains [attorney's mental impressions and conclusions / work
product prepared in anticipation of this litigation]."
```

---

### Other Privileges

**Spousal Privilege (TRE 504):**
- Confidential communications between spouses during marriage
- Note: Often waived in divorce context

**Accountant-Client Privilege (Texas Occupations Code):**
- Limited privilege for communications with CPA

**Clergy-Communicant Privilege (TRE 505):**
- Communications to clergy in professional capacity

**Physician-Patient Privilege (TRE 509):**
- Medical records and communications
- Often subject to exception in family law cases

## Snap-Back/Clawback Provision

**TRCP 193.3(d)** - Inadvertent Production

If privileged documents are inadvertently produced:

1. Notify receiving party promptly
2. Request return or destruction
3. Receiving party must:
   - Return or destroy documents
   - Not use or disclose until privilege resolved
   - May seek court ruling on privilege

**Sample Clawback Request:**
```
Dear Counsel:

Upon review, we have determined that [document description] was
inadvertently produced in discovery. This document is protected by
[attorney-client privilege / work product doctrine].

Pursuant to TRCP 193.3(d), please immediately return all copies of
this document and confirm that you have not disclosed or used the
document.

[Attorney Name]
```
