# Example: Trial Strategy Development

## User Request

```
Help me develop trial strategy for this contested custody case:

Case: Smith v. Smith, Cause No. 2025-54321
Court: 257th District Court, Harris County
Trial Date: March 15, 2026

Our Client: Sarah Smith (Mother/Petitioner)
Opposing Party: Michael Smith (Father/Respondent)

Children:
- Emma Smith, age 10
- Jacob Smith, age 7

Current Situation:
- Parties separated 8 months ago
- Temporary orders: JMC, Mother primary, Father standard possession
- Children have been with Mother since separation
- Children attend Greenwood Elementary (in Mother's neighborhood)

Mother's Position:
- Seeking to maintain primary residence
- Wants to keep children in current school
- Alleging Father has anger issues and drinks excessively
- Children have expressed preference to stay with Mother

Father's Position:
- Seeking primary residence or 50/50 schedule
- Denies alcohol problem
- Claims Mother alienates children against him
- Wants children to attend school in his neighborhood

Key Evidence:
1. Text messages from Father admitting he "had too much to drink" on multiple occasions
2. School records showing consistent attendance under Mother's care
3. Therapist notes (children have been seeing counselor)
4. Police report from noise complaint at Father's home (no arrest)
5. Father's DWI from 2023 (prior to separation)
6. Social media posts showing Father at bars frequently
7. Declaration from Mother's sister (witnessed Father intoxicated around children)

Expert:
- Dr. Patricia Wells conducted custody evaluation
- Recommends Mother as primary
- Notes Father's alcohol consumption as concern

What's my trial strategy?
```

## Expected Analysis

### 1. Case Theory Development

**Theme:** "Stability and safety for Emma and Jacob"

**Core Narrative:** The children have thrived under Mother's primary care during separation. They are excelling in school, have established friendships, and have stability in their routine. Father's alcohol issues, documented by his own admissions and independent witnesses, create an unsafe environment that is not in the children's best interest.

### 2. Best Interest Factor Analysis

| Factor | Our Evidence | Their Likely Argument | Response |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| Desires of child | Emma (10) preference | Children coached | Age-appropriate, consistent statements |
| Emotional/physical needs | School success, therapy engagement | Mother overreacts | Professional supports our concerns |
| Danger | DWI, alcohol admissions, police report | Isolated incidents | Pattern of behavior |
| Parental abilities | 8 months successful primary care | Mother alienates | Children have normal relationship with Father during visits |
| Stability | Current arrangement working | Father can provide | No evidence Father's home more stable |

### 3. Witness Order

1. **Sarah Smith (Client)** - 60 min
   - Establish history as primary caretaker
   - Document Father's alcohol incidents
   - Show children's current thriving
   - Authenticate text messages

2. **Dr. Patricia Wells (Expert)** - 45 min
   - Qualifications
   - Methodology
   - Findings regarding Father's alcohol use
   - Best interest recommendation

3. **Jessica Martinez (Mother's Sister)** - 20 min
   - Witnessed Father intoxicated
   - Corroborate Mother's testimony
   - Observations of children

4. **Michael Smith (Adverse)** - 45 min cross
   - Lock in alcohol admissions
   - Impeach with text messages
   - DWI admission
   - Social media contradictions

### 4. Exhibit Strategy

| Exhibit | Authentication | Purpose | Witness |
|---------|---------------|---------|---------|
| P-1: Text messages | Distinctive characteristics | Alcohol admissions | Sarah Smith |
| P-2: DWI records | Certified public record | Prior conviction | Self-authenticating |
| P-3: Police report | Certified public record | Pattern of issues | Self-authenticating |
| P-4: School records | Business record | Children thriving | Custodian or 902(10) |
| P-5: Social media screenshots | Distinctive characteristics | Bar attendance | Sarah Smith |
| P-6: Expert report | Expert testimony | Best interest opinion | Dr. Wells |
| P-7: Sister's declaration | Live testimony | Corroboration | Jessica Martinez |

### 5. Opening Statement Theme

"Your Honor, this case is about what is best for Emma and Jacob Smith. The evidence will show that for the past eight months, these children have thrived in their mother's care. They are succeeding in school. They have stability in their routine. And they are safe.

The evidence will also show that their father, Michael Smith, has a problem with alcohol. You will see text messages where he admits to drinking too much. You will hear testimony about incidents where he was intoxicated around the children. And you will hear from Dr. Patricia Wells, a custody evaluator, who recommends that the children remain primarily with their mother.

At the end of this trial, we will ask the Court to continue the current arrangement that has allowed these children to thrive."

### 6. Anticipated Defense and Rebuttal

| Their Argument | Our Rebuttal |
|----------------|--------------|
| Mother is alienating | Children have positive relationship during Father's possession |
| Alcohol issues exaggerated | Father's own admissions, expert opinion, multiple witnesses |
| Father equally capable | No track record; Mother has 8 months proven success |
| Children should be in Father's school | No evidence that school is superior; stability matters |

### 7. Closing Argument Framework

Apply evidence to each best interest factor:

1. **Desires of child** - Emma clearly expressed preference (age 10 = weight to her statements)
2. **Emotional needs** - Children in therapy, doing well under current arrangement
3. **Physical danger** - Father's alcohol use documented; DWI shows judgment issues
4. **Parental abilities** - Mother: proven 8 months. Father: concerns about judgment
5. **Stability** - Current arrangement IS stability

**Request:** Maintain current orders - JMC, Mother primary with geographic restriction, Father standard possession.

### 8. Risk Assessment

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Expert cross on methodology | Pre-prepare expert on Daubert challenges |
| Father presents sympathetically | Focus on specific incidents, not character attack |
| Children's statements excluded | Age 10 = generally reliable; have therapist backup |
| Alienation claim gains traction | Evidence of Father's time with children; no interference |

### 9. Contingency Planning

**If Court inclined toward 50/50:**
- Propose expanded standard instead
- Request alcohol monitoring
- Request structured exchange location

**If Court questions Mother's flexibility:**
- Highlight all possession time honored
- Show communication attempts documented
