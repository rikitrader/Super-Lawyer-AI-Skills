# Workflow 11: Enforcement and Modification

## Purpose

Enforce existing court orders when the opposing party fails to comply, or modify orders when material and substantial changes in circumstances warrant adjustment. This workflow covers contempt proceedings, enforcement actions, and modification procedures.

## Phase

Post-Decree

## Inputs Required

| Input | Source | Required |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Existing court orders | Prior proceedings | Yes |
| Violation documentation | Client/evidence | For enforcement |
| Changed circumstances | Client/evidence | For modification |
| Current financial information | Client | For support modification |

## Outputs Produced

| Output | Destination | Format |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Motion for Enforcement | Court filing | PDF |
| Motion to Modify | Court filing | PDF |
| Contempt findings | Court | PDF |
| Modified orders | Court | PDF |

## Timeline and Deadlines

| Event | Deadline | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|

| Service of citation | Before hearing | Personal service required for contempt |
| Modification requirements | Material change exists | No waiting period |

## Part A: Enforcement Proceedings

### Enforcement Overview

**Types of Enforcement:**

| Order Type | Enforcement Method |
|------------|-------------------|
| Child support | Contempt, wage withholding, liens |
| Possession/visitation | Contempt, make-up time |
| Property division | Contempt, clarification, partition |
| Spousal maintenance | Contempt, wage withholding |
| Injunctions | Contempt |

### Step A1: Document the Violation

**Violation Documentation Checklist:**
- [ ] Identify specific order violated
- [ ] Date(s) of violation
- [ ] Nature of violation
- [ ] Evidence of violation
- [ ] Witnesses to violation
- [ ] Damage or impact of violation

**Evidence Types by Violation:**

*Child Support Arrearage:*
- [ ] Payment records (SDU, direct payments)
- [ ] Arrearage calculation
- [ ] Employment records
- [ ] Bank records

*Possession Violation:*
- [ ] Communication records (texts, emails)
- [ ] Calendar showing missed time
- [ ] Witness statements
- [ ] Police reports (if applicable)

*Property Violation:*
- [ ] Deed/title records
- [ ] Account statements
- [ ] Correspondence demanding compliance
- [ ] Timeline of non-compliance

### Step A2: Demand for Compliance

**Before Filing:**
- [ ] Send written demand letter
- [ ] Specify violation
- [ ] Demand specific compliance
- [ ] Set deadline for compliance
- [ ] Warn of enforcement action

**Demand Letter Elements:**
```
RE: Enforcement of Court Orders
    Cause No. {{CAUSE_NO}}

Dear [Opposing Party/Counsel]:

This letter concerns your failure to comply with the
[order type] entered on [date] in the above-referenced
cause.

Specifically, you have failed to:
[List specific violations]

You are hereby demanded to:
[List specific compliance required]

If compliance is not achieved by [date], my client will
have no alternative but to file a Motion for Enforcement
seeking contempt findings and all available remedies.

[Signature]
```

### Step A3: Prepare Motion for Enforcement

**Motion for Enforcement Requirements:**

*Caption and Style*

*Allegations:*
1. Prior orders exist (attach certified copy)
2. Respondent had notice of orders
3. Respondent had ability to comply
4. Respondent failed to comply
5. Specific violations (date, nature)
6. Damages/impact of violation

*Relief Requested:*
- [ ] Finding of contempt
- [ ] Jail time (suspended or actual)
- [ ] Arrearage judgment
- [ ] Make-up possession time
- [ ] Attorney's fees
- [ ] Court costs

**Contempt Requirements:**
```
To hold someone in contempt, must prove:
1. Valid court order existed
2. Respondent had knowledge of order
3. Respondent had ability to comply
4. Respondent willfully failed to comply

Burden: Beyond reasonable doubt (criminal contempt)
        Clear and convincing (civil contempt)
```

### Step A4: Service Requirements

**Personal Service Required:**
- Contempt is quasi-criminal
- Must be personally served
- Cannot serve by mail or posting
- Respondent must appear

**Citation Must Include:**
- Copy of motion
- Notice of hearing date
- Warning of potential jail

### Step A5: Enforcement Hearing

**Hearing Preparation:**
- [ ] Organize all evidence
- [ ] Prepare witness testimony
- [ ] Calculate arrearages
- [ ] Prepare proposed findings
- [ ] Prepare proposed order

**Hearing Procedure:**
1. Movant presents evidence of violation
2. Respondent may present defenses
3. Court makes findings
4. Court imposes remedies

**Defenses to Contempt:**
- Inability to comply (not willful)
- Ambiguous order
- Compliance achieved
- Justification/excuse

### Step A6: Enforcement Remedies

**Available Remedies:**

*For Child Support:*
- Judgment for arrearage
- Wage withholding order
- License suspension
- Passport denial
- Credit reporting
- Contempt (up to 6 months per violation)
- Community supervision

*For Possession Violations:*
- Make-up possession time
- Modified possession schedule
- Contempt
- Bond requirement

*For Property Violations:*
- Order of delivery
- Court executes deed
- Partition and sale
- Contempt

## Part B: Modification Proceedings

### Modification Overview

**Grounds for Modification:**

| Order Type | Modification Standard |
|------------|----------------------|
| Conservatorship | Material and substantial change |
| Possession | Material and substantial change |
| Child support | 3 years + 20% change, OR material change |
| Spousal maintenance | Material and substantial change |
| Geographic restriction | Material and substantial change |

### Step B1: Evaluate Modification Grounds

**Material and Substantial Change Analysis:**

*Child-Related Modifications:*
```
COMMON GROUNDS:
□ Change in child's needs
□ Change in parenting circumstances
□ Child reaching certain age
□ Parent relocation
□ Safety concerns emerged
□ Change in parent's work schedule
□ Child's preference (if 12+)
□ Parent's lifestyle change
```

*Support Modifications:*
```
CHILD SUPPORT MODIFICATION GROUNDS:
□ 3 years since order + 20% change would result, OR
□ Material and substantial change including:
  - Obligor income change (increase or decrease)
  - Obligee income change
  - Child's needs changed
  - Number of children changed
  - Medical insurance changed
```

*Spousal Maintenance Modifications:*
```
GROUNDS TO MODIFY:
□ Obligor's resources decreased
□ Obligee's resources increased
□ Obligee cohabitation
□ Other material change

GROUNDS TO TERMINATE:
□ Obligee remarriage
□ Obligee cohabitation (certain circumstances)
□ Obligee death
□ Obligor death
```

### Step B2: Gather Supporting Evidence

**For Custody Modification:**
- [ ] Evidence of changed circumstances
- [ ] Evidence of best interest factors
- [ ] Child's current needs
- [ ] Current parenting arrangement issues
- [ ] School records
- [ ] Medical records
- [ ] Witness statements

**For Support Modification:**
- [ ] Current income documentation
- [ ] Current expense documentation
- [ ] Change in circumstances evidence
- [ ] Updated child support calculation

### Step B3: Prepare Petition to Modify

**Petition Requirements:**

*Caption and Style*

*Allegations:*
1. Prior orders exist (date, court)
2. Jurisdiction continues
3. Material and substantial change
4. Specific changes alleged
5. Best interest of child (if applicable)
6. Requested modifications

*Relief Requested:*
- [ ] Modify conservatorship
- [ ] Modify possession/access
- [ ] Modify child support
- [ ] Modify spousal maintenance
- [ ] Other specific modifications
- [ ] Attorney's fees

### Step B4: Temporary Orders Pending Modification

**If Immediate Relief Needed:**
- [ ] Motion for Temporary Orders
- [ ] Show immediate need
- [ ] Request interim relief
- [ ] Maintain status quo where appropriate

### Step B5: Modification Discovery

**Focused Discovery:**
- [ ] Updated financial disclosure
- [ ] Employment records
- [ ] Updated expenses
- [ ] School/medical records (children)
- [ ] Third-party depositions if needed

### Step B6: Modification Hearing/Trial

**Hearing Preparation:**
- [ ] Evidence of change
- [ ] Evidence of best interest
- [ ] Updated calculations
- [ ] Proposed modified order

**Presentation:**
1. Establish prior orders
2. Prove material change
3. Prove modification warranted
4. Prove best interest (children)
5. Present proposed new terms

### Step B7: Modified Orders

**Upon Modification:**
- [ ] Review modified order
- [ ] Ensure accurate terms
- [ ] Calculate effective dates
- [ ] Update child support withholding
- [ ] Calendar new obligations

## Risk Flags

| Risk | Indicator | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Weak contempt case | Ability to comply questionable | Consider civil remedies only |
| Modification denied | Insufficient change shown | Strengthen evidence or wait |
| Retroactive limits | Support modification timing | File promptly, understand limits |
| Venue issues | Parties moved | Verify proper venue |
| Statute of limitations | Old violations | Check applicable limits |

## Courtroom Notes

- Contempt is serious—judges require clear proof
- Keep meticulous records of violations
- Document all communication attempts
- Modification requires proving CHANGE, not just disagreement
- Child support modifications are often limited retroactively
- Best interest of child is paramount in custody modifications
- Courts prefer cooperation over conflict
