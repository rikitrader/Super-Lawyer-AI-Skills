## Timeline Reference

### Uncontested Divorce
- Total duration: 61-90 days
- Minimum: 60-day waiting period
- If agreed: Can finalize day after waiting period

### Contested Divorce (Moderate)
- Total duration: 6-12 months
- Discovery: 2-4 months
- Mediation: Required
- Trial: 1-3 days

### Contested Divorce (Complex)
- Total duration: 12-24 months
- Extensive discovery
- Multiple experts
- Trial: 3-5+ days

### SAPCR/Modification
- Total duration: 4-12 months
- Depends on complexity
- Mediation required
- Hearing: Half-day to 2 days

### Enforcement/Contempt
- Total duration: 2-6 months
- Expedited if incarceration sought
- Hearing: Half-day to 1 day

### Protective Order
- Temporary Ex Parte: Same day/next day
- Full Hearing: Within 14 days
- Duration: Up to 2 years

## System Classification Criteria

### A-System (Aggressive Pursuit)

**Criteria:**
- Primary caregiver with documented involvement
- Other party has history of violence, substance abuse, or neglect
- Clear separate property with documentation
- Financial records show hidden assets or waste
- Strong witness support
- Prior court findings in your favor

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: 70%+
- Settlement leverage: High
- Temporary orders: Should be aggressive

**Strategy:**
- File first to control narrative
- Seek favorable temporary orders immediately
- Use discovery offensively
- Consider sole managing conservatorship
- Push for early mediation from position of strength

### B-System (Balanced Approach)

**Criteria:**
- Both parents actively involved
- No clear "fault" issues
- Property requires valuation/tracing
- Disputed facts on key issues
- Both parties have credibility

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: 40-70%
- Settlement leverage: Moderate
- Temporary orders: Status quo likely

**Strategy:**
- Thorough discovery to build case
- Develop evidence through experts
- Prepare for mediation as resolution point
- Be ready for trial but seek settlement
- Consider joint managing conservatorship

### C-System (Conservative/Defensive)

**Criteria:**
- History of issues (substance abuse, violence, neglect)
- Other party was primary caregiver
- Limited documentation of involvement
- Financial misconduct on your side
- Credibility concerns

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: <40%
- Settlement leverage: Low
- Temporary orders: Protect what you can

**Strategy:**
- Focus on preserving parental relationship
- Accept reasonable possession schedule
- Protect separate property aggressively
- Settle early to limit exposure
- Demonstrate rehabilitation/change

- Monthly cap: $9,200 (subject to annual adjustment)

4. Travel costs for possession
5. Positive/negative cash flow from property
6. Special needs of child
7. Child care expenses
8. Healthcare costs not covered by insurance
9. Extraordinary educational expenses
10. Significant income of child

## Spousal Maintenance Quick Reference

### Eligibility (Tex. Fam. Code § 8.051)

Must establish ONE of:
1. **Family Violence**: Committed within 2 years before filing or during pendency
2. **Disability**: Spouse or child has incapacitating disability
3. **Long Marriage**: Married 10+ years AND lacks ability to earn sufficient income
4. **Criminal Conviction**: Spouse convicted of family violence during proceedings

### Duration Limits (§ 8.054)

| Marriage Duration | Maximum Maintenance |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| <10 years (violence) | 5 years |
| 10-20 years | 5 years |
| 20-30 years | 7 years |
| 30+ years | 10 years |
| Disability (self or child) | Indefinite |

### Amount Limitations (§ 8.055)
- Maximum: $5,000/month OR 20% of obligor's gross income
- Whichever is LESS

## Property Division Framework

### Community Property Presumption
- All property acquired during marriage presumed community
- Burden on party claiming separate property
- Clear and convincing evidence standard for tracing

### Separate Property Categories
1. Property owned before marriage
2. Property acquired by gift
3. Property acquired by inheritance
4. Personal injury recovery (except lost wages)

### Reimbursement Claims
- Community funds used for separate property benefit
- Separate funds used for community property benefit
- Reduction of separate property debt with community funds
- Capital improvements to separate property

### "Just and Right" Factors (§ 7.001)
Courts consider:
1. Spouses' capacities and abilities
2. Benefits innocent spouse would have received
3. Business opportunities
4. Education and training
5. Relative physical condition
6. Relative financial condition
7. Disparity of ages
8. Size of separate estates
9. Nature of property
10. Fault in breakup
11. Wasting of community assets
12. Tax consequences
13. Attorney's fees
